<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
<p><b>REN21</b>, the <b>Renewable Energy Policy Network for the 21st Century</b>, is a global <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> multi-stakeholder policy network that provides international leadership for the rapid transition to renewable energy. The network was launched in June 2004 as an outcome of the International Conference for Renewable Energies in <a href="/wiki/Bonn" title="Bonn">Bonn</a>, Germany. REN21 brings together governments, nongovernmental organisations, research and academic institutions, international organisations and industry to learn from one another and build on successes that advance renewable energy. To assist policy decision making, REN21 provides high quality information, catalyses discussion and debate and supports the development of thematic networks.</p>
<p>REN21 has produced a number of internationally recognised reports on renewable energy policy, industry and market development. Since 2005 it has produced its annual flagship publication, theRenewables Global Status Report (GSR). The GSR provides a comprehensive overview of renewable energy market, industry, investment and policy development worldwide. Data is collected from a network of more than 500 contributors and researchers. REN21 also produces regional analyses such as regional reports for <a href="/wiki/MENA" title="MENA">MENA</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ECOWAS" title="ECOWAS">ECOWAS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southern_African_Development_Community" title="Southern African Development Community">SADC</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_African_Community" title="East African Community">EAC</a>. The range of credible possibilities on the future of renewable energy is detailed in the publication Renewables Global Futures Report. Up-to-date country data on renewable energy can be accessed through REN21's Interactive Map. This map was created to facilitate the accessibility of renewable energy knowledge to a broad range of stakeholders in the energy sector and beyond.</p>
<p>REN21 works in cooperation with other organisations. A sister report to REN21’s GSR is the Global Trends in Renewable Energy Investment Report (GTR) produced by theFrankfurt School – <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Environment_Programme" title="United Nations Environment Programme">UNEP</a> Collaborating Centre for Climate &amp; Sustainable Energy Finance. REN21 is also a partner of the Global Tracking Framework, which is convened by the UN Secretary General’s Sustainable Energy for All (SE4All) Initiative. REN21 also collaborates with other intergovernmental organisations such as the <a href="/wiki/International_Energy_Agency" title="International Energy Agency">IEA</a>, the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_Renewable_Energy_Agency" title="International Renewable Energy Agency">IRENA</a>.</p>
<p>Initiated at the renewables 2004 conference in Bonn, <a href="/wiki/International_Renewable_Energy_Conference" title="International Renewable Energy Conference">IRECs</a> a high-level political conference series dedicated to renewable energy policy worldwide. Dedicated exclusively to the renewable energy sector, IRECs are hosted by alternate Governments every two years and convened by REN21. REN21’s role in the IREC process is as follows:</p>
<p>• Establishment of an International Advisory Committee (IAC) and hosting of its meetings on a frequency to be agreed to by the Parties</p>
<p>• Development of the IREC conference programme in collaboration with the host country</p>
<p>• Facilitation of the production of the IREC Conference Declaration and outcomes</p>
<p>• Promoting IREC on a global level through the REN21 network and its associated events</p>
<p>These multi-stakeholder events act as a common platform for government, private sector and civil society leaders to jointly address the goal of advancing renewable energy and have provided the impulse for several momentous initiatives over the past decade. IREC have served as motor in the respective host country (2004: Germany; 2005: China; 2008: USA; 2010: India; 2013: United Arab Emirates; 2015: South Africa).</p>
<p>The REN21 secretariat is based at UNEP (United Nations Environment Programme) in Paris, France and is a registered non-profit association under German law (e.V.). When established in Paris in 2006, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Hugo_Suding" title="Paul Hugo Suding">Paul Hugo Suding</a>, was the first Head of Secretariat. Since formalization <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Christine_Lins&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Christine Lins (page does not exist)">Christine Lins</a> is Executive Secretary.</p>
