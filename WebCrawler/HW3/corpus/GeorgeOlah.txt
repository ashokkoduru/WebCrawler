<p><b>George Andrew Olah</b> (born <b>Oláh György</b>; May 22, 1927) is a <a href="/wiki/Hungarians" title="Hungarians">Hungarian</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Chemist" title="Chemist">chemist</a>. His research involves the generation and reactivity of <a href="/wiki/Carbocation" title="Carbocation">carbocations</a> via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Superacids" title="Superacids">superacids</a>. For this research, Olah was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Chemistry" title="Nobel Prize in Chemistry">Nobel Prize in Chemistry</a> in 1994. He has also been awarded the <a href="/wiki/Priestley_Medal" title="Priestley Medal">Priestley Medal</a>, the highest honor granted by the <a href="/wiki/American_Chemical_Society" title="American Chemical Society">American Chemical Society</a> and <a href="/wiki/F._Albert_Cotton#F.A._Cotton_Medal_for_Excellence_in_Chemical_Research" title="F. Albert Cotton">F.A. Cotton Medal for Excellence in Chemical Research</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_Chemical_Society" title="American Chemical Society">American Chemical Society</a> in 1996.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_early_life">Education and early life</span></h2>
<p>Olah was born in <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, Hungary, on May 22, 1927, to Magda (Krasznai) and Julius Oláh, a lawyer. After the high school of Budapesti Piarista Gimnazium (<a href="/wiki/Piarists" title="Piarists">Scolopi fathers</a>), he studied, then taught, at what is now <a href="/wiki/Budapest_University_of_Technology_and_Economics" title="Budapest University of Technology and Economics">Budapest University of Technology and Economics</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_and_research">Career and research</span></h2>
<p>As a result of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1956_Hungarian_Revolution" title="1956 Hungarian Revolution">1956 Hungarian Revolution</a>, he and his family moved briefly to England and then to Canada, where he joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dow_Chemical" title="Dow Chemical">Dow Chemical</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sarnia" title="Sarnia">Sarnia</a>, Ontario, with another Hungarian chemist, Stephen J. Kuhn. Olah's pioneering work on carbocations started during his eight years with Dow. In 1965 he returned to academia at <a href="/wiki/Case_Western_Reserve_University" title="Case Western Reserve University">Case Western Reserve University</a> and then to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a> in 1977. In 1971, Olah became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naturalized_citizen" title="Naturalized citizen">naturalized citizen</a> of the United States.</p>
<p>As of April 2016, Olah is a distinguished professor at the University of Southern California and the director of the <a href="/wiki/Loker_Hydrocarbon_Research_Institute" title="Loker Hydrocarbon Research Institute">Loker Hydrocarbon Research Institute</a>. In 2005, Olah wrote an essay promoting the <a href="/wiki/Methanol_economy" title="Methanol economy">methanol economy</a>.</p>
<p>The Olah family formed an <a href="/wiki/Financial_endowment" title="Financial endowment">endowment fund</a> (the George A. Olah Endowment) which grants annual awards to outstanding chemists. The awards are selected and administered by the <a href="/wiki/American_Chemical_Society" title="American Chemical Society">American Chemical Society</a>.</p>
<p>The search for stable <a href="/wiki/Carbocation" title="Carbocation">carbocations</a> led to the discovery of protonated <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a> which was stabilized by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Superacids" title="Superacids">superacids</a>, like FSO<sub>3</sub>H-SbF<sub>5</sub> ("<a href="/wiki/Magic_acid" title="Magic acid">Magic Acid</a>").</p>

<p>Olah was also involved in a career-long battle with <a href="/wiki/Herbert_C._Brown" title="Herbert C. Brown">Herbert C. Brown</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Purdue" title="Purdue">Purdue</a> over the existence of so-called "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-classical_ion" title="Non-classical ion">nonclassical</a>" carbocations – such as the <a href="/wiki/Norbornane" title="Norbornane">norbornyl</a> cation, which can be depicted as cationic character delocalized over several bonds.</p>
<p>In recent years, his research has shifted from <a href="/wiki/Hydrocarbon" title="Hydrocarbon">hydrocarbons</a> and their transformation into fuel to the <a href="/wiki/Methanol_economy" title="Methanol economy">methanol economy</a>. He has joined with <a href="/wiki/Robert_Zubrin" title="Robert Zubrin">Robert Zubrin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anne_Korin" title="Anne Korin">Anne Korin</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Woolsey" title="James Woolsey">James Woolsey</a> in promoting a flexible-fuel mandate initiative.</p>
