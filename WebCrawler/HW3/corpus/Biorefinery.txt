<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>A <b>biorefinery</b> is a facility that integrates biomass conversion processes and equipment to produce fuels, power, heat, and value-added chemicals from <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">biomass</a>. The biorefinery concept is analogous to today's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Petroleum_refinery" title="Petroleum refinery">petroleum refinery</a>, which produce multiple fuels and products from <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/International_Energy_Agency" title="International Energy Agency">International Energy Agency</a> Bioenergy Task 42 on Biorefineries has defined biorefining as the sustainable processing of biomass into a spectrum of bio-based products (food, feed, chemicals, materials) and bioenergy (biofuels, power and/or heat).</p>
<p>By producing multiple products, a biorefinery takes advantage of the various components in biomass and their intermediates therefore maximizing the value derived from the biomass feedstock. A biorefinery could, for example, produce one or several low-volume, but high-value, chemical or <a href="/wiki/Nutraceutical" title="Nutraceutical">nutraceutical</a> products and a low-value, but high-volume liquid transportation fuel such as <a href="/wiki/Biodiesel" title="Biodiesel">biodiesel</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bioethanol" title="Bioethanol">bioethanol</a> (see also <a href="/wiki/Alcohol_fuel" title="Alcohol fuel">alcohol fuel</a>). At the same time generating electricity and process heat, through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Combined_heat_and_power" title="Combined heat and power">combined heat and power</a> (CHP) technology, for its own use and perhaps enough for sale of electricity to the local utility. The high-value products increase profitability, the high-volume fuel helps meet energy needs, and the power production helps to lower energy costs and reduce <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emissions from traditional power plant facilities. Although some facilities exist that can be called bio-refineries, the bio-refinery has yet to be fully realized. Future biorefineries may play a major role in producing chemicals and materials that are traditionally produced from <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span></h2>
<p>The fully operational <a href="/wiki/Blue_Marble_Energy" title="Blue Marble Energy">Blue Marble Energy</a> company has multiple biorefineries located in Odessa, WA and Missoula, MT.</p>
<p>Canada's first Integrated Biorefinery, developed on anaerobic digestion technology by <a href="/wiki/Himark_BioGas" title="Himark BioGas">Himark BioGas</a> is located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hairy_Hill,_Alberta" title="Hairy Hill, Alberta">Hairy Hill, Alberta</a>. The Biorefinery utilizes Source Separated Organics from the metro <a href="/wiki/Edmonton" title="Edmonton">Edmonton</a> region, Open Pen Feedlot Manure, and Food Processing Waste.</p>
<p>Several potential biorefinery examples have been proposed, starting from feedstocks such as <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flax" title="Flax">flax</a> <a href="/wiki/Straw" title="Straw">straw</a> and the residues from the production of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bioethanol" title="Bioethanol">bioethanol</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Chemrec" title="Chemrec">Chemrec's</a> technology for <a href="/wiki/Black_liquor" title="Black liquor">black liquor</a> <a href="/wiki/Gasification" title="Gasification">gasification</a> and production of <a href="/wiki/Second-generation_biofuels" title="Second-generation biofuels">second-generation biofuels</a> such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biomethanol" title="Biomethanol">biomethanol</a> or Bio<a href="/wiki/Dimethyl_ether" title="Dimethyl ether">DME</a> is integrated with a host <a href="/wiki/Pulp_mill" title="Pulp mill">pulp mill</a> and utilizes a major <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sulfate_process" title="Sulfate process">sulfate</a> or <a href="/wiki/Sulfite_process" title="Sulfite process">sulfite process</a> waste product as feedstock.</p>
