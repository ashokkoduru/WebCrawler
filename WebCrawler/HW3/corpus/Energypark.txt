<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p>An <b>energy park</b> is a separate area used and planned for the purpose of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clean_energy" title="Clean energy">clean energy</a> development, like <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind</a> and <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">solar</a> generation facilities.</p>
<p>Energy parks create many other economic development benefits too. In <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, energy parks are creating thousands of <a href="/wiki/Green_job" title="Green job">green jobs</a>. In Minnesota, community wind parks are also popular. In England, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wind_park" title="Wind park">wind parks</a> are commonly known as <a href="/wiki/Wind_farm" title="Wind farm">wind farms</a>. A more "lightweight" version of an energy park is a wind park or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_park" title="Solar park">solar park</a>. These have one type of clean energy generation, rather than two or more technologies, as in an energy park.</p>
<p>Some energy parks feature additional features beyond clean energy generation. Additional benefits include: green job creation, <a href="/wiki/Smart_grid" title="Smart grid">Smart grid</a> connections, as well as new recreational, technology innovation and agricultural opportunities. The Stamford Energy Park in Vermont is one example of an integrated energy park.</p>
