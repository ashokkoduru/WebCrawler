<p>The <b>Environmental Sustainability Index</b> (ESI) was a composite index published from 1999 to 2005 that tracked 21 elements of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_sustainability" title="Environmental sustainability">environmental sustainability</a> covering natural resource endowments, past and present <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a> levels, environmental management efforts, contributions to protection of the <a href="/wiki/Commons" title="Commons">global commons</a>, and a society's capacity to improve its environmental performance over time.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p>The ESI was published between 1999 to 2005 by <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University's</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yale_Center_for_Environmental_Law_and_Policy" title="Yale Center for Environmental Law and Policy">Center for Environmental Law and Policy</a> in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University's</a> Center for International Earth Science Information Network (CIESIN), and the <a href="/wiki/World_Economic_Forum" title="World Economic Forum">World Economic Forum</a>.</p>
<p>The Environmental Sustainability Index was developed to evaluate environmental sustainability relative to the paths of other countries. Due to a shift in focus by the teams developing the ESI, a new index was developed, the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_Performance_Index" title="Environmental Performance Index">Environmental Performance Index</a> (EPI), that uses outcome-oriented indicators, then working as a benchmark index that can be more easily used by policy makers, environmental scientists, advocates and the general public. The EPI has been published in 2006, 2008, and 2010.</p>
<p>In 2010 Iceland was noted by <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a> as "The Greenest Country", reaching highest score by the Environmental Sustainability Index with a score of 93.5/100.</p>
