<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="hatnote" role="note">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Environmental_science" title="Environmental science">Environmental science</a>.</div>


<p><b>Environmental studies</b> is a multidisciplinary academic field which systematically studies <a href="/wiki/Human_behavior" title="Human behavior">human interaction</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environment</a> in the interests of solving complex problems. Environmental studies brings together the principles of sciences, commerce/ economics and social sciences so as to solve contemporary environmental problems. It is a broad field of study that includes the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">natural environment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Built_environment" title="Built environment">built environment</a>, and the sets of relationships between them. The field encompasses study in basic principles of <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_science" title="Environmental science">environmental science</a>, as well as associated subjects such as <a href="/wiki/Environmental_ethics" title="Environmental ethics">ethics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_geography" title="Environmental geography">geography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_policy" title="Environmental policy">policy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_politics" title="Environmental politics">politics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_law" title="Environmental law">law</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_economics" title="Environmental economics">economics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_philosophy" title="Environmental philosophy">philosophy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_sociology" title="Environmental sociology">environmental sociology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_justice" title="Environmental justice">environmental justice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_planning" title="Environmental planning">planning</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pollution_control" title="Pollution control">pollution control</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource_management" title="Natural resource management">natural resource management</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/State_University_of_New_York_College_of_Environmental_Science_and_Forestry" title="State University of New York College of Environmental Science and Forestry">New York State College of Forestry at Syracuse University</a> established a BS in environmental studies degree in the 1950s, awarding its first degree in 1956. <a href="/wiki/Middlebury_College" title="Middlebury College">Middlebury College</a> established the major there in 1965.</p>
<p>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_Studies_Association_of_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Environmental Studies Association of Canada (page does not exist)">Environmental Studies Association of Canada</a> (ESAC) was established in 1993 "to further research and teaching activities in areas related to environmental studies in Canada". ESAC's magazine, <i><a href="/wiki/A%5CJ:_Alternatives_Journal" title="A\J: Alternatives Journal">A\J: Alternatives Journal</a></i> was first published by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_A._Paehlke&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Robert A. Paehlke (page does not exist)">Robert A. Paehlke</a> on 4 July 1971.</p>
<p>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_Environmental_Studies_and_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Association for Environmental Studies and Sciences (page does not exist)">Association for Environmental Studies and Sciences</a> (AESS) was founded in 2008 as the first professional association in the interdisciplinary field of environmental studies in the United States. The Association's scholarly journal, the <i>Journal of Environmental Studies and Sciences</i> (JESS), commenced publication in March 2011.</p>
