<p>The <b>World Climate Research Programme</b> (WCRP) was established in 1980, under the joint sponsorship of <a href="/wiki/International_Council_for_Science" title="International Council for Science">International Council for Science</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>, and has also been sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_Oceanographic_Commission" title="Intergovernmental Oceanographic Commission">Intergovernmental Oceanographic Commission</a> of <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> since 1993. It is a component of the <a href="/wiki/World_Climate_Programme" title="World Climate Programme">World Climate Programme</a>. The objectives of the programme are to develop the fundamental scientific understanding of the physical <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a> system and climate processes needed to determine to what extent climate can be predicted and the extent of human influence on climate. The programme encompasses studies of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere" title="Earth's atmosphere">global atmosphere</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">oceans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sea_ice" title="Sea ice">sea ice</a>, land ice (such as <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">glaciers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_cap" title="Ice cap">ice caps</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ice_sheet" title="Ice sheet">ice sheets</a>), and the land surface which together constitute the Earth's physical climate system.</p>
<p>WCRP activities address issues of scientific uncertainty in the Earth's climate system including transport and storage of heat by the ocean, the global energy and <a href="/wiki/Water_cycle" title="Water cycle">hydrological cycle</a>, the formation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clouds" title="Clouds">clouds</a> and their effects on radiative transfer, and the role of the <a href="/wiki/Cryosphere" title="Cryosphere">cryosphere</a> in climate. These activities match the scientific priorities identified by the <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change" title="Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change">Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change</a>, and provide the basis for responding to issues raised in the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Framework_Convention_on_Climate_Change" title="United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change">United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change</a>. WCRP also lays the scientific foundation for meeting the research challenges posed in Agenda 21. Together with the <a href="/wiki/International_Geosphere-Biosphere_Programme" title="International Geosphere-Biosphere Programme">International Geosphere-Biosphere Programme</a> and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=International_Human_Dimensions_of_Global_Environmental_Change_Programme&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="International Human Dimensions of Global Environmental Change Programme (page does not exist)">International Human Dimensions of Global Environmental Change Programme</a>, WCRP provides the international framework for scientific cooperation in the study of global <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>.</p>
<p>Scientific guidance for the programme is provided by a Joint Scientific Committee consisting of 18 scientists selected by mutual agreement between the three sponsoring organizations.</p>
