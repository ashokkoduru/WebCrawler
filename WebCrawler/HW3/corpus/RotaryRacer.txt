<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>Rotary Racer</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Greenpower" title="Greenpower">Greenpower</a> team which started in 2005 as an initiative within <a href="/wiki/Chipping_Sodbury_School" title="Chipping Sodbury School">Chipping Sodbury School</a> to try to get 'Lads and Dads' working together on a project to build and race their own electric racing cars. Ever since the team has grown, with 9 different cars being built within the past 9 years. The team are made up of both girls and boys, ranging between the ages of 11 and 18.</p>

<p>The name Rotary Racer came from the team's first sponsors -<a href="/wiki/Rotary_International" title="Rotary International">Rotary International</a> of Chipping Sodbury. This charity provided the funding needed to start the car from scratch and, ever since, have continued to sponsor the team each new season. Over the years, even more companies have sponsored the team so they are able to continue racing as well as improve their car. The current sponsors are:</p>

<p>Since 2005, the team have won the national championship (held at <a href="/wiki/Goodwood_Circuit" title="Goodwood Circuit">Goodwood Circuit</a> or <a href="/wiki/Rockingham_Motor_Speedway" title="Rockingham Motor Speedway">Rockingham Motor Speedway</a>) five times out of the eight years.</p>
<ul>
<li>2008 2nd in the Country</li>
<li>2009 National Champions</li>
<li>2010 3rd in the Country</li>
<li>2011 National Champions</li>
<li>2012 National Champions</li>
<li>2013 National Champions</li>
<li>2014 2nd Internationally</li>
<li>2015 International Champions</li>
</ul>
<p>Rotary Racer take part in both the two 1 hour endurance races (F24) and the F24+ race (for sixth formers).</p>
