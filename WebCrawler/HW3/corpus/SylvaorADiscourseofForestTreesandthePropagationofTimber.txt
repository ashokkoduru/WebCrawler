<p><i><b>Sylva, or A Discourse of Forest-Trees and the Propagation of Timber in His Majesty's Dominions</b></i> by the English writer <a href="/wiki/John_Evelyn" title="John Evelyn">John Evelyn</a> was first presented in 1662 as a paper to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a>. It was published as a book two years later in 1664, and is recognised as one of the most influential texts on <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a> ever published.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Editions">Editions</span></h2>
<ul>
<li>1662 <i>Sylva</i> <b>paper</b> was presented to the Royal Society on 16 February 1662.</li>
<li>1664 <i>Sylva</i> First Edition <b>book</b> printed by publisher <a href="/wiki/John_Martyn_(publisher)" title="John Martyn (publisher)">John Martyn</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a>, and the first book published after the granting of their Royal Charter as publishers in 1662.</li>
<li>1670 <i>Sylva</i> Second Edition. Various engravings added.</li>
<li>1679 <i>Sylva</i> Third Edition. Included an essay from Evelyn about soils: <i>Terra, a Philosophical Essay of Earth, being a Lecture in Course</i>.</li>
<li>1706 <i>Silva</i> Fourth Edition, now spelt <i><b>Silva</b></i>, contained new sections <i>Dendrologia</i>, <i>Pomona; Or, An Appendix concerning Fruit-Trees in relation to CIDER</i> and <i>Kalendarium Hortense</i>. This was the last edition during Evelyn's lifetime.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_reproductions">Recent reproductions</span></h3>
<ul>
<li>A facsimile of the first edition (1664) was produced in 1972 by the publisher <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Scolar_Press&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Scolar Press (page does not exist)">Scolar Press</a>.</li>
<li>The fourth edition (1706) was republished in 1908 by Doubleday &amp; Co. with a foreword by John Nisbet. This 1908 edition was republished in facsimile by Kessinger Publishing (30 Nov 2007).</li>
<li>A new edition by <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Hemery" title="Gabriel Hemery">Gabriel Hemery</a> with illustrations by <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Simblet" title="Sarah Simblet">Sarah Simblet</a> is published by <a href="/wiki/Bloomsbury" title="Bloomsbury">Bloomsbury</a> to coincide with the 350th anniversary in 2014 of the book's first publication. It is titled <i>The New Sylva: a discourse of forest and orchard trees for the twenty-first century</i>. The authors have their own blog following the book's creation: <a class="external text" href="http://www.newsylva.com" rel="nofollow">The New Sylva</a></li>
</ul>
