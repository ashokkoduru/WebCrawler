<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Environmental quality</b> is a set of properties and characteristics of the environment, either generalized or local, as they impinge on human beings and other organisms. It is a measure of the condition of an environment relative to the requirements of one or more species and or to any human need or purpose.</p>
<p>Environmental quality is a general term which can refer to varied characteristics that relate to the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">natural environment</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Built_environment" title="Built environment">built environment</a>, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_quality" title="Air quality">air</a> and <a href="/wiki/Water_quality" title="Water quality">water purity</a> or <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a>, <a href="/wiki/Noise" title="Noise">noise</a> and the potential effects which such characteristics may have on physical and mental health.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span></h2>
<p>In the United States, the term is applied with a body of federal and state <a href="/wiki/Regulation" title="Regulation">standards and regulations</a> that are monitored by regulatory agencies. All states in the U.S. have some form of a department or commission that is responsible for a variety of activities such as monitoring quality, responding to citizen complaints, and enforcing environmental regulations. The agency with the lead implementation responsibility for most major federal environmental laws (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Clean_Air_Act_(United_States)" title="Clean Air Act (United States)">Clean Air Act</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clean_Water_Act" title="Clean Water Act">Clean Water Act</a>) is the US <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a> (EPA). Other federal agencies with significant oversight roles include the <a href="/wiki/Council_on_Environmental_Quality" title="Council on Environmental Quality">Council on Environmental Quality</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Interior" title="United States Department of Interior">Department of the Interior</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers#Environmental_enforcement" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">Army Corps of Engineers</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span></h2>
<p>In the United Kingdom, the environment has been the primary responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Environment,_Food_and_Rural_Affairs" title="Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs">Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs</a> (DEFRA). Predecessor bodies were merged in 2001 to create this department with a broader remit to link rural activities to the natural environment. Some responsibilities are devolved to the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish Government</a> and are exercised by the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Environment_Protection_Agency" title="Scottish Environment Protection Agency">Scottish Environment Protection Agency</a> (SEPA) and the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_for_Wales" title="National Assembly for Wales">National Assembly for Wales</a>, while delivery of environmental initiatives often use partners, including: <a href="/wiki/British_Waterways" title="British Waterways">British Waterways</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environment_Agency" title="Environment Agency">Environment Agency</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forestry_Commission" title="Forestry Commission">Forestry Commission</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Natural_England" title="Natural England">Natural England</a>. DEFRA also has a remit to oversee impacts of activities within the built environment and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Climate_Change_Programme" title="United Kingdom Climate Change Programme">United Kingdom Climate Change Programme</a>.</p>
