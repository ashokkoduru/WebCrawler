<p><b>Mixed farming</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Agrarian_system" title="Agrarian system">agrarian system</a> that comprises farming along with the raising of livestock simultaneously. When on a farm along-with crop production, some other agriculture based practice like poultry, dairy farming or bee keeping etc. is adopted, then this system of farming is known as mixed farming. It is the dominant system in Europe and now in parts of India, where most farms have a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Field_(agriculture)" title="Field (agriculture)">fields</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pasture" title="Pasture">pastures</a>.It was first mainly used for self-consumption, but now in Advanced countries like USA, Japan, etc., this is done for a commercial purpose</p>
<p>For example, the same farm may grow cereal crops, and keep cattle, sheep, pigs or poultry.</p>
<p>In mixed farming, along with farming some other agriculture based practices are also carried out.</p>
<p>Often the dung from the cattle is used to fertilize the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cereal_crop" title="Cereal crop">cereal crops</a>. Before horses were used for haulage, many young male cattle were often not butchered as surplus for meat but castrated and used as <a href="/wiki/Ox" title="Ox">bullocks</a> to haul the cart and the plough.</p>
