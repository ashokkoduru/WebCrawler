<p><b>Motorsport</b> or <b>motorsports</b> is a global term used to encompass the group of competitive events which primarily involve the use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motor_vehicles" title="Motor vehicles">motorised vehicles</a>, whether for racing or non-racing competition. The <a href="/wiki/Terminology" title="Terminology">terminology</a> can also be used to describe forms of competition of two-wheeled motorised vehicles under the banner of <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_racing" title="Motorcycle racing">motorcycle racing</a>, and includes off-road racing such as <a href="/wiki/Motocross" title="Motocross">motocross</a>.</p>
<p>Four- (or more) wheeled motorsport competition is globally governed by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA); and the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_Motocyclisme" title="Fédération Internationale de Motocyclisme">Fédération Internationale de Motocyclisme</a> (FIM) governs two-wheeled competition.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p>In 1894, a French newspaper organised a race from <a href="/wiki/Paris%E2%80%93Rouen_(motor_race)" title="Paris–Rouen (motor race)">Paris to Rouen</a> and back, starting city to city racing. In 1900, the <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Bennett_Cup_(auto_racing)" title="Gordon Bennett Cup (auto racing)">Gordon Bennett Cup</a> was established. Closed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Circuit_racing" title="Circuit racing">circuit racing</a> arose as open road racing, on public roads, was banned. <a href="/wiki/Brooklands" title="Brooklands">Brooklands</a> was the first dedicated motor racing track in the United Kingdom.</p>
<p>Following <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, European countries organised <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_motor_racing" title="Grand Prix motor racing">Grand Prix races</a> over closed courses. In the United States, dirt track racing became popular.</p>
<p>After World War II, the Grand Prix circuit became more formally organised. In the United States, <a href="/wiki/Stock_car_racing" title="Stock car racing">stock car racing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drag_racing" title="Drag racing">drag racing</a> became firmly established.</p>
<p>Motorsports ultimately became divided by types of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motor_vehicles" title="Motor vehicles">motor vehicles</a> into racing events, and their appropriate organisations.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Open-wheel_racing"><span id="Racing"></span><span id="Motor_racing"></span> Open-wheel racing</span></h2>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Open-wheel_car" title="Open-wheel car">Open-wheel car</a></div>
<p>Open-wheel racing is a set of classes of motor vehicles, with their wheels outside of, and not contained by any bodywork of their vehicle. These have been globally classified as specific 'Formula' series - the most common being <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>, and many others include the likes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Formula_3" title="Formula 3">Formula 3</a>, <a href="/wiki/Formula_Ford" title="Formula Ford">Formula Ford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Formula_Renault" title="Formula Renault">Formula Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/Formula_Palmer_Audi" title="Formula Palmer Audi">Formula Palmer Audi</a>. However, in North America, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IndyCar" title="IndyCar">IndyCar</a> series is their pinnacle open-wheeled racing series. More recently, new open-wheeled series have been created, originating in Europe, which omit the 'Formula' moniker, such as <a href="/wiki/GP2_Series" title="GP2 Series">GP2</a> and <a href="/wiki/GP3_Series" title="GP3 Series">GP3</a>. Former 'Formula' series include <a href="/wiki/Formula_5000" title="Formula 5000">Formula 5000</a> and <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formula_One">Formula One</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> is a class of single-seat and <a href="/wiki/Open-wheel_car" title="Open-wheel car">open-wheel</a> grand prix closed course racing, governed by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA), and currently organized by the privately owned company <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Group" title="Formula One Group">Formula One Group</a>. The formula regulations contain a very strict set of rules which govern vehicle power, weight and size.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="IndyCar_Series">IndyCar Series</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/IndyCar_Series" title="IndyCar Series">IndyCar Series</a></div>
<p>In the United States, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indy_Car" title="Indy Car">Indy Car</a> is a class of single seat paved track racing, organized by <a href="/wiki/INDYCAR" title="INDYCAR">INDYCAR</a>. Its premier race is the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_500" title="Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Enclosed_wheel_racing">Enclosed wheel racing</span></h2>

<p>Enclosed wheel racing is a set of classes of vehicles, where the wheels are primarily enclosed inside the bodywork of the vehicle, similar to a North American 'stock car'.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_car_racing">Sports car racing</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sports_car_racing" title="Sports car racing">Sports car racing</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sports_car_racing" title="Sports car racing">Sports car racing</a> is a set of classes of vehicles, over a closed course track, including sports cars, and specialised racing types. The premiere race is the <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Le Mans</a> which takes place annually in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> during the month of June. Sports car racing rules and specifications differentiate in North America from established international sanctioning bodies.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stock_car_racing">Stock car racing</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Stock_car_racing" title="Stock car racing">Stock car racing</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Stock_car_racing" title="Stock car racing">Stock car racing</a> is a set of vehicles, that race over a speedway track, organised by <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a>. While once stock cars, the vehicles are now purpose built, but resemble the body design and shape of production cars. NASCAR was organised in 1947, to combine flat track oval racing of production cars. <a href="/wiki/Daytona_Beach_and_Road_Course" title="Daytona Beach and Road Course">Daytona Beach and Road Course</a> was founded where land speed records were set on the beach, and including part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A1A" title="A1A">A1A</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Touring_car_racing">Touring car racing</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Touring_car_racing" title="Touring car racing">Touring car racing</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Touring_car_racing" title="Touring car racing">Touring car racing</a> is a set of vehicles, modified street cars, that race over closed purpose built race tracks and street courses.</p>
