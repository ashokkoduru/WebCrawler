<p>The following <a href="/wiki/Outline_(list)" title="Outline (list)">outline</a> is provided as an overview of and topical guide to solar energy:</p>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">Solar energy</a></b> – radiant <a href="/wiki/Light" title="Light">light</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heat" title="Heat">heat</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">sun</a>. It has been harnessed by humans since <a href="/wiki/Ancient_history" title="Ancient history">ancient times</a> using a range of ever-evolving technologies. Solar energy technologies include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_heating" title="Solar heating">solar heating</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_photovoltaics" title="Solar photovoltaics">solar photovoltaics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_thermal_electricity" title="Solar thermal electricity">solar thermal electricity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Solar_architecture" title="Solar architecture">solar architecture</a>, which can make considerable contributions to solving some of the most urgent problems the world now faces.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="What_type_of_thing_is_solar_energy.3F">What <i>type</i> of thing is solar energy?</span></h2>
<p>Solar energy can be described as all of the following:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">Energy</a> – an indirectly observed quantity, often understood as the ability a physical system has to do work on other physical systems.
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">Renewable energy</a> – energy which comes from natural resources which are naturally replenished.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">Natural resource</a> – materials and components (something that can be used) that can be found within the environment. Every man-made product is composed of natural resources (at its fundamental level). A natural resource may exist as a separate entity such as fresh water, and air, as well as a living organism such as a fish, or it may exist in an alternate form which must be processed to obtain the resource such as metal ores, oil, and most forms of energy.
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Renewable_resource" title="Renewable resource">Renewable resource</a> – natural resource with the ability to reproduce through biological or natural processes and are replenished with the passage of time.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sunlight">Sunlight</span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> – the star at the center of the Solar System</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">Sunlight</a> – in the broad sense, is the total frequency spectrum of electromagnetic radiation given off by the Sun, particularly infrared, visible, and ultraviolet light.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conversion_of_solar_energy">Conversion of solar energy</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Solar_power" title="Solar power">Solar power</a> – the conversion of sunlight into electricity, either directly using photovoltaics (PV), or indirectly using concentrated solar power (CSP).</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Active_solar" title="Active solar">Active solar</a> – technologies are employed to convert solar energy into another more useful form of energy (for example, to heat or electrical energy).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Heliostat" title="Heliostat">Heliostat</a> – a device that includes a mirror, usually a plane mirror, which turns so as to keep reflecting sunlight toward a predetermined target, compensating for the sun's apparent motions in the sky.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_tracker" title="Solar tracker">Solar tracker</a> – devices that orient various payloads toward the sun.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Copper_in_renewable_energy" title="Copper in renewable energy">Role of copper</a> in generating and transmitting energy with maximum efficiency in solar photovoltaic power generation, concentrating solar thermal power, and solar water heaters.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_cell_efficiency" title="Solar cell efficiency">Solar cell efficiency</a> –</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_cell" title="Solar cell">Solar cell</a> – made from a monocrystalline silicon wafer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_chemical" title="Solar chemical">Solar chemical</a> – a number of possible processes that harness solar energy by absorbing sunlight in a chemical reaction in a way similar to photosynthesis in plants but without using living organisms.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_constant" title="Solar constant">Solar constant</a> – a measure of flux density, is the amount of incoming solar electromagnetic radiation per unit area that would be incident on a plane perpendicular to the rays, at a distance of one astronomical unit|astronomical unit (AU) (roughly the mean distance from the Sun to the Earth).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_inverter" title="Solar inverter">Solar inverter</a> – or PV inverter, converts the variable direct current output of a photovoltaic (PV) solar panel into a utility frequency alternating current that can be fed into a commercial electrical grid or used by a local, off-grid electrical network.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_photovoltaics" title="Solar photovoltaics">Solar photovoltaics</a> –</li>
<li class="mw-empty-elt"></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_thermal_energy">Solar thermal energy</span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Solar_thermal_energy" title="Solar thermal energy">Solar thermal energy</a> (STE) – technology for harnessing solar energy for thermal energy (heat).</p>
<ul>
<li>Solar district heating and solar district cooling. See: <a href="/wiki/Seasonal_thermal_energy_storage" title="Seasonal thermal energy storage">Seasonal thermal energy storage</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_water_heating" title="Solar water heating">Solar water heating</a> (SWH) – or solar hot water (SHW) systems comprise several innovations and many mature renewable energy technologies that have been well established for many years.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_air_conditioning" title="Solar air conditioning">Solar air conditioning</a> – any air conditioning (cooling) system that uses solar power.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thermal_mass" title="Thermal mass">Thermal mass</a> – a concept in building design regarding how the mass of the building provides "inertia" against temperature fluctuations, sometimes known as the thermal flywheel effect.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_pond" title="Solar pond">Solar pond</a> – a pool of saltwater which acts as a large-scale solar thermal energy collector with integral heat storage for supplying thermal energy.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_chimney" title="Solar chimney">Solar chimney</a> – solar chimney – often referred to as a thermal chimney – is a way of improving the natural ventilation of buildings by using convection of air heated by passive solar energy.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Concentrated_solar_power">Concentrated solar power</span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Concentrated_solar_power" title="Concentrated solar power">Concentrated solar power</a> – a system that uses mirrors or lenses to concentrate a large area of sunlight, or solar thermal energy, onto a small area. Electrical power is produced when the concentrated light is converted to heat, which drives a heat engine (usually a steam turbine) connected to an electrical power generator.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Parabolic_trough" title="Parabolic trough">Parabolic trough</a> – a type of solar thermal energy collector.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/Photoelectric_effect" title="Photoelectric effect">Photoelectric effect</a> – electrons are emitted from matter (metals and non-metallic solids, liquids or gases) as a consequence of their absorption of energy from electromagnetic radiation of very short wavelength, such as visible or ultraviolet radiation.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Photovoltaics" title="Photovoltaics">Photovoltaics</a> (PV) – is a method of generating electrical power by converting solar radiation into direct current electricity using semiconductors that exhibit the photovoltaic effect.</li>

<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_generation_photovoltaic_cell" title="Third generation photovoltaic cell">Third generation photovoltaic cell</a> – are solar cells that are potentially able to overcome the Shockley-Queisser limit of 31-41% power efficiency for single bandgap solar cells.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_cell_research" title="Solar cell research">Solar cell research</a> – in universities and research institutions around the world.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quantum_efficiency_of_a_solar_cell" title="Quantum efficiency of a solar cell">Quantum efficiency of a solar cell</a> –</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cadmium_telluride" title="Cadmium telluride">Cadmium telluride</a> – a crystalline compound formed from cadmium and tellurium, used as an infrared optical window and a solar cell material.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thermophotovoltaic" title="Thermophotovoltaic">Thermophotovoltaic</a> (TPV) – energy conversion is a direct conversion process from heat differentials to electricity via photons.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polycrystalline_silicon_photovoltaics" title="Polycrystalline silicon photovoltaics">Polycrystalline silicon photovoltaics</a> – are a type of solar cell.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thermodynamic_efficiency_limit" title="Thermodynamic efficiency limit">Thermodynamic efficiency limit</a> – the absolute maximum theoretically possible conversion efficiency of sunlight to electricity.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sun-free_photovoltaics" title="Sun-free photovoltaics">Sun-free photovoltaics</a> –</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Polarizing_organic_photovoltaics" title="Polarizing organic photovoltaics">Polarizing organic photovoltaics</a> (ZOPV) – is a concept for harvesting energy from Liquid crystal display screens, developed by engineers from UCLA.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_cell" title="Solar cell">Solar cell</a> – made from a monocrystalline silicon wafer.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Polymer_solar_cell" title="Polymer solar cell">Polymer solar cell</a> – are a type of flexible solar cell.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nanocrystal_solar_cell" title="Nanocrystal solar cell">Nanocrystal solar cell</a> – are solar cells based on a substrate with a coating of nanocrystals.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_panel" title="Solar panel">Solar panel</a> – (also solar module, photovoltaic module or photovoltaic panel) is a packaged, connected assembly of photovoltaic cells.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Photovoltaic_system" title="Photovoltaic system">Photovoltaic system</a> – (PV system) use solar panels to convert sunlight into electricity.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Photovoltaic_power_station" title="Photovoltaic power station">Photovoltaic power station</a> – also known as a solar park or solar farm, is a large-scale photovoltaic system designed for the supply of merchant power into the electricity grid.</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Experimental_proposed_solar_power">Experimental proposed solar power</span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_updraft_tower" title="Solar updraft tower">Solar updraft tower</a> – a renewable-energy power plant for generating electricity from solar power.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar-pumped_laser" title="Solar-pumped laser">Solar-pumped laser</a> – laser that shares the same optical properties as conventional lasers such as emitting a beam consisting of coherent electromagnetic radiation which can reach high power, but which uses solar radiation for pumping the lasing medium. This type of laser is unique from other types in that it does not require any artificial energy source.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thermoelectric_generator" title="Thermoelectric generator">Thermoelectric generator</a> – (also called thermogenerators) are devices which convert heat (temperature differences) directly into electrical energy, using a phenomenon called the "Seebeck effect" (or "thermoelectric effect").</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_chemical" title="Solar chemical">Solar chemical</a> – a number of possible processes that harness solar energy by absorbing sunlight in a chemical reaction in a way similar to photosynthesis in plants but without using living organisms.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Space-based_solar_power" title="Space-based solar power">Space-based solar power</a> – : Part of the solar energy is lost on its way through the atmosphere by the effects of reflection and absorption.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_sail" title="Solar sail">Solar sail</a> – (also called light sails or photon sails) are a form of spacecraft propulsion using the radiation pressure (also called solar pressure) of light from a star to push large ultra-thin mirrors to high speeds.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Magnetic_sail" title="Magnetic sail">Magnetic sail</a> – or magsail is a proposed method of spacecraft propulsion which would use a static magnetic field to deflect charged particles radiated by the Sun as a plasma wind, and thus impart momentum to accelerate the spacecraft.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Solar_thermal_rocket" title="Solar thermal rocket">Solar thermal rocket</a> – a theoretical spacecraft propulsion system that would make use of solar power to directly heat reaction mass, and therefore would not require an electrical generator like most other forms of solar-powered propulsion do.</li>
</ul>
