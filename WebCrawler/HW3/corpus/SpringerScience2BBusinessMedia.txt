<p><b>Springer Science+Business Media</b> or <b>Springer</b> is a global <a href="/wiki/Publishing" title="Publishing">publishing</a> company that publishes books, <a href="/wiki/E-book" title="E-book">e-books</a> and peer-reviewed journals in science, technical and medical (STM) publishing. Springer also hosts a number of scientific databases, including SpringerLink, <a href="/wiki/Springer_Protocols" title="Springer Protocols">Springer Protocols</a>, and SpringerImages. Book publications include major reference works, textbooks, monographs and book series; more than 168,000 titles are available as e-books in 24 subject collections. Springer has major offices in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg" title="Heidelberg">Heidelberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dordrecht" title="Dordrecht">Dordrecht</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.</p>
<p>On January 15, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Holtzbrinck_Publishing_Group" title="Holtzbrinck Publishing Group">Holtzbrinck Publishing Group</a> / <a href="/wiki/Nature_Publishing_Group" title="Nature Publishing Group">Nature Publishing Group</a> and Springer Science+Business Media announced a merger. The transaction was concluded in May 2015 and a new joint venture company, <a href="/wiki/Springer_Nature" title="Springer Nature">Springer Nature</a>, was formed, with Holtzbrinck having the majority 53% share and BC Partners retaining 47% interest in the company.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Julius_Springer" title="Julius Springer">Julius Springer</a> founded <b>Springer-Verlag</b> in Berlin in 1842 and his son Ferdinand Springer grew it from a small firm of 4 employees into Germany's second largest academic publisher with 65 staff in just 30 years. In 1964, Springer expanded its business internationally, opening an office in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. Offices in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a> soon followed.</p>
<p>The academic publishing company <b>BertelsmannSpringer</b> was formed after <a href="/wiki/Bertelsmann" title="Bertelsmann">Bertelsmann</a> bought a majority stake in Springer-Verlag in 1999. The British investment groups <a href="/wiki/Cinven" title="Cinven">Cinven</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Candover" title="Candover">Candover</a> bought BertelsmannSpringer from Bertelsmann in 2003. They merged the company in 2004 with the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> publisher <b>Kluwer Academic Publishers</b> which they bought from <a href="/wiki/Wolters_Kluwer" title="Wolters Kluwer">Wolters Kluwer</a> in 2002, to form <b>Springer Science+Business Media.</b></p>
<p>Springer acquired the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_access_(publishing)" title="Open access (publishing)">open-access publisher</a> <a href="/wiki/BioMed_Central" title="BioMed Central">BioMed Central</a> in October 2008 for an undisclosed amount.</p>
<p>In 2009, Cinven and Candover sold Springer to two private equity firms, <a href="/wiki/EQT_Partners" title="EQT Partners">EQT Partners</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_Singapore_Investment_Corporation" title="Government of Singapore Investment Corporation">Government of Singapore Investment Corporation</a>. The closing of the sale was confirmed in February 2010 after the competition authorities in the USA and in Europe approved the transfer.</p>
<p>In 2011, Springer acquired Pharma Marketing and Publishing Services from Wolters Kluwer.</p>
<p>In 2013, the London-based private equity firm <a href="/wiki/BC_Partners" title="BC Partners">BC Partners</a> acquired a majority stake in Springer from EQT and GIC for $4.4 billion.</p>
<p>In 2014, it was revealed that Springer had published 16 fake papers in its journals that had been computer-generated using <a href="/wiki/SCIgen" title="SCIgen">SCIgen</a>. Springer subsequently removed all the papers from these journals. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IEEE" title="IEEE">IEEE</a> had also done the same thing by removing more than 100 fake papers from its conference proceedings.</p>
<p>In 2015, Springer <a href="/wiki/Retraction" title="Retraction">retracted</a> 64 of the papers it had published after it was found that they had gone through a fraudulent peer review process.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electronic_products">Electronic products</span></h2>
<p>Springer provides its electronic book and journal content on its SpringerLink site, which launched in 1996.</p>
<p>SpringerProtocols is home to a collection of protocols, recipes which provide step-by-step instructions for conducting experiments in research labs.</p>
<p>SpringerImages was launched in 2008 and offers a collection of currently 1.8 million images spanning science, technology, and medicine.</p>
<p>SpringerMaterials was launched in 2009 and is a platform for accessing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landolt-B%C3%B6rnstein" title="Landolt-Börnstein">Landolt-Börnstein</a> database of research and information on materials and their properties.</p>
<p>AuthorMapper is a free online tool for visualizing scientific research that enables document discovery based on author locations and geographic maps. The tool helps users explore patterns in scientific research, identify literature trends, discover collaborative relationships, and locate experts in several scientific/medical fields.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Open_access">Open access</span></h2>
<p>For some of its journals, Springer does not require its authors to transfer their copyrights, and allows them to decide whether their articles are published under an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_access_(publishing)" title="Open access (publishing)">open-access</a> license or in the traditional restricted license model. While open-access publishing typically requires the author to pay a fee for copyright retention, this fee is sometimes covered by a third party. For example, a national institution in Poland allows authors to publish in open-access journals without incurring any personal cost - but using public funds. Springer is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Open_Access_Scholarly_Publishers_Association" title="Open Access Scholarly Publishers Association">Open Access Scholarly Publishers Association</a>.</p>
