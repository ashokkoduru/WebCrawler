<p><b>Pasture</b> (from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>pastus</i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Past_participle" title="Past participle">past participle</a> of <i>pascere</i>, "to feed") is land used for <a href="/wiki/Grazing" title="Grazing">grazing</a>.</p>
<p>Pasture lands in the narrow sense are enclosed tracts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Farmland" title="Farmland">farmland</a>, grazed by domesticated <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">livestock</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swine" title="Swine">swine</a>. The vegetation of tended pasture, <a href="/wiki/Forage" title="Forage">forage</a>, consists mainly of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grasses" title="Grasses">grasses</a>, with an interspersion of <a href="/wiki/Legume" title="Legume">legumes</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Forb" title="Forb">forbs</a> (non-grass herbaceous plants). Pasture is typically grazed throughout the summer, in contrast to <a href="/wiki/Meadow" title="Meadow">meadow</a> which is ungrazed or used for grazing only after being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mowing" title="Mowing">mown</a> to make <a href="/wiki/Hay" title="Hay">hay</a> for animal <a href="/wiki/Fodder" title="Fodder">fodder</a>. Pasture in a wider sense additionally includes <a href="/wiki/Rangeland" title="Rangeland">rangelands</a>, other unenclosed <a href="/wiki/Pastoralism" title="Pastoralism">pastoral systems</a>, and land types used by wild animals for <a href="/wiki/Grazing" title="Grazing">grazing</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Browsing_(predation)" title="Browsing (predation)">browsing</a>.</p>
<p>Pasture lands in the narrow sense are distinguished from rangelands by being managed through more intensive agricultural practices of <a href="/wiki/Sowing" title="Sowing">seeding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a>, and the use of <a href="/wiki/Fertilizer" title="Fertilizer">fertilizers</a>, while rangelands grow primarily native vegetation, managed with extensive practices like <a href="/wiki/Controlled_burn" title="Controlled burn">controlled burning</a> and regulated intensity of grazing.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Soil" title="Soil">Soil</a> type, minimum annual <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainfall" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a> are important factors in pasture management. Sheepwalk is an area of <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a> where <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a> can roam freely. The productivity of sheepwalk is measured by the number of sheep per area. This is dependent, among other things, on the underlying rock. Sheepwalk is also the name of townlands in <a href="/wiki/County_Roscommon" title="County Roscommon">County Roscommon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Fermanagh" title="County Fermanagh">County Fermanagh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>.</p>
<p>Unless <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Factory_farming" title="Factory farming">factory farming</a>, which entails in its most intensive form entirely trough-feeding, managed or unmanaged pasture is the main food source for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ruminants" title="Ruminants">ruminants</a>. Pasture feeding dominates livestock farming where the land makes crop sowing and/or harvesting difficult, such as in arid or mountainous regions, where types of <a href="/wiki/Camel" title="Camel">camel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">goat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antelope" title="Antelope">antelope</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yak" title="Yak">yak</a> and other ruminants live which are well suited to the more hostile terrain and very rarely factory farmed. In more humid regions, pasture grazing is managed across a large global area for <a href="/wiki/Free_range" title="Free range">free range</a> and <a href="/wiki/Organic_farming" title="Organic farming">organic farming</a>. Certain types of pasture suit the diet, <a href="/wiki/Natural_selection" title="Natural selection">evolution</a> and metabolism of particular animals, and their fertilising and tending of the land may over generations result in the pasture combined with the ruminants in question being integral to a particular <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a>.</p>
