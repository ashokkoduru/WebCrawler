<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
<p><b>Agribusiness</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">business</a> of agricultural production. The term was coined in 1957 by Goldberg and Davis. It includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agrichemical" title="Agrichemical">agrichemicals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Animal_husbandry" title="Animal husbandry">breeding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crop" title="Crop">crop</a> production (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Farming" title="Farming">farming</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contract_farming" title="Contract farming">contract farming</a>), distribution, <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_machinery" title="Agricultural machinery">farm machinery</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Processed_food" title="Processed food">processing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seed</a> supply, as well as <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">marketing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Retail" title="Retail">retail</a> sales. All agents of the food and fiber value chain and those institutions that influence it are part of the agribusiness system.</p>
<p>Within the agriculture <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industry</a>, "agribusiness" is used simply as a <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a> of agriculture and business, referring to the range of activities and disciplines encompassed by modern food production. There are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academic_degrees" title="Academic degrees">academic degrees</a> in and departments of agribusiness, agribusiness <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trade_associations" title="Trade associations">trade associations</a>, agribusiness <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Publications" title="Publications">publications</a>, and so forth, worldwide.</p>
<p>The UN's <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Agriculture_Organization" title="Food and Agriculture Organization">Food and Agriculture Organization</a> (FAO) operates a section devoted to Agribusiness Development which seeks to promote food industry growth in developing nations.</p>
<p>In the context of agribusiness management in academia, each individual element of agriculture production and distribution may be described as agribusinesses. However, the term "agribusiness" most often emphasizes the "interdependence" of these various sectors within the production chain.</p>
<p>Among critics of large-scale, industrialized, <a href="/wiki/Vertical_integration" title="Vertical integration">vertically integrated</a> food production, the term <i>agribusiness</i> is used negatively, synonymous with <i><a href="/wiki/Corporate_farming" title="Corporate farming">corporate farming</a></i>. As such, it is often contrasted with smaller <a href="/wiki/Family_farm" title="Family farm">family-owned farms</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span></h2>
<p>Examples of agribusinesses include seed and agrichemical producers like <a href="/wiki/Dow_AgroSciences" title="Dow AgroSciences">Dow AgroSciences</a>, <a href="/wiki/DuPont" title="DuPont">DuPont</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monsanto" title="Monsanto">Monsanto</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Syngenta" title="Syngenta">Syngenta</a>; AB Agri (part of <a href="/wiki/Associated_British_Foods" title="Associated British Foods">Associated British Foods</a>) animal feeds, biofuels, and micro-ingredients, <a href="/wiki/Archer_Daniels_Midland" title="Archer Daniels Midland">ADM</a>, grain transport and processing; <a href="/wiki/John_Deere" title="John Deere">John Deere</a>, farm machinery producer; <a href="/wiki/Ocean_Spray_(cooperative)" title="Ocean Spray (cooperative)">Ocean Spray</a>, farmer's cooperative; and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Purina_Farms" title="Purina Farms">Purina Farms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agritourism" title="Agritourism">agritourism</a> farm.</p>
<p>As concern over <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> intensifies, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biofuels" title="Biofuels">biofuels</a> derived from crops are gaining increased public and scientific attention. This is driven by factors such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_price_increases_since_2003" title="Oil price increases since 2003">oil price spikes</a>, the need for increased <a href="/wiki/Energy_security" title="Energy security">energy security</a>, concern over <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emissions from <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">fossil fuels</a>, and support from <a href="/wiki/Ethanol_fuel_in_the_United_States#Tax_credits" title="Ethanol fuel in the United States">government subsidies</a>. In Europe and in the US, increased research and production of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biofuels" title="Biofuels">biofuels</a> has been mandated by law.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Studies_and_reports">Studies and reports</span></h2>
<p>Studies of agribusiness often come from the academic fields of <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_economics" title="Agricultural economics">agricultural economics</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Management_studies" title="Management studies">management studies</a>, sometimes called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Agribusiness_management&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Agribusiness management (page does not exist)">agribusiness management</a>. To promote more development of food economies, many government agencies support the research and publication of economic studies and reports exploring agribusiness and agribusiness practices. Some of these studies are on foods produced for export and are derived from agencies focused on food exports. These agencies include the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Agricultural_Service" title="Foreign Agricultural Service">Foreign Agricultural Service</a> (FAS) of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Agriculture" title="U.S. Department of Agriculture">U.S. Department of Agriculture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agriculture_and_Agri-Food_Canada" title="Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada">Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada</a> (AAFC), <a href="/wiki/Austrade" title="Austrade">Austrade</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Trade_and_Enterprise" title="New Zealand Trade and Enterprise">New Zealand Trade and Enterprise</a> (NZTE). The <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_International_Trade_Associations" title="Federation of International Trade Associations">Federation of International Trade Associations</a> publishes studies and reports by FAS and AAFC, as well as other non-governmental organizations on its website.</p>
<p>Ray A. Goldberg coined the term agribusiness together with coauthor <a href="/wiki/John_H._Davis_(diplomat)" title="John H. Davis (diplomat)">John H. Davis</a>. They provided a rigorous economic framework for the field in their book A Concept of Agribusiness (Boston: Division of Research, Graduate School of Business Administration, Harvard University, 1957). That seminal work traces a complex value-added chain that begins with the farmer's purchase of seed and livestock and ends with a product fit for the consumer's table. Agribusiness boundary expansion is driven by a variety of <a href="/wiki/Transaction_cost" title="Transaction cost">transaction costs</a>.</p>
<p>Manuel Alvarado Ledesma (CEMA University, Argentina) and Peter D. Goldsmith (University of Illinois) explain the implications of weak institutions on agribusiness investment. According to them weak institutions lead to policy development and enforcement grounded in the moment, rather than based on precedent and deliberative processes over time.</p>
