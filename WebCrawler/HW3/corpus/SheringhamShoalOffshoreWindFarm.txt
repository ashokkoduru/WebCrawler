<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
<p><b>Sheringham Shoal Offshore Wind Farm</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Round_2_wind_farm" title="Round 2 wind farm">Round 2 wind farm</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> off the coast of Norfolk. A lease for use of the sea bed was obrained in 2004 by Scira Offshore Energy (later acquired by <a href="/wiki/Statoil" title="Statoil">Statoil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Statkraft" title="Statkraft">Statkraft</a>), the development gained offshore planning consent in 2008, and was constructed 2009-2011, being officially opened in 2012.</p>
<p>The windfarm has 88 <a href="/wiki/Siemens_Wind_Power" title="Siemens Wind Power">Siemens Wind Power</a> 3.6MW turbines (total power 316.8 MW) spread over a 35 km (14 sq mi) area over 17 km (11 mi) from shore.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p>In 2004 the <a href="/wiki/Crown_Estate" title="Crown Estate">Crown Estate</a> awarded Econventures (Utrecht, NL) the lease of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Round_2_wind_farm" title="Round 2 wind farm">Round 2 wind farm</a> site at Sheringham shoal. Econventures together with SLP Energy (Lowestoft, UK) formed a joint venture <i>Scira Offshore Energy</i> to develop a c. 315MW wind farm. Development work for Econventures was to be carried out by Evelop BV, both subsidiaries of <a href="/wiki/Econcern" title="Econcern">Econcern BV</a>. In 2005 <a href="/wiki/Norsk_Hydro" title="Norsk Hydro">Hydro</a> took a 50% stake in Scira, acquiring 25% shareholdings from both SLP Energy and Ecocentures.</p>
<p>In 2006 Scira submitted a planning application to the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Trade_and_Industry_(United_Kingdom)" title="Department of Trade and Industry (United Kingdom)">Department of Trade and Industry</a> for a 108 turbine, 315 MW wind farm.</p>
<p>The planned wind farm was approximately 18 kilometres (11 mi) off the coast of Norfolk at <a href="/wiki/Sheringham" title="Sheringham">Sheringham</a>, just within the 12 nm UK territorial water boundary, and 5 kilometres (3.1 mi) north of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sand_bank" title="Sand bank">sand bank</a> known as Sheringham shoal. The wind farm would be located at water depths of 16 to 22 metres (52 to 72 ft) and consist of somewhere between 45 and 108 turbines. Benefits of the site included low shipping and trawling intensities; lack of any dredging, dumping, oil/gas, or MoD practice areas, and of cables or pipelines; as well as low visual impact from the coast, and outside any nature conservation areas. The seabed at the windfarm and offshore cable route consisted of mainly gravely sand, overlying chalk. The electrical power export cable was to be connected to a switching station near <a href="/wiki/Muckleburgh_Collection" title="Muckleburgh Collection">Muckleburgh Collection</a>, via landfall near <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Weybourne_Hope&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Weybourne Hope (page does not exist)">Weybourne Hope</a>. Two routes were considered for the export cable, one avoiding the sand bank atSheringham shoal. The connection to the <a href="/wiki/National_Grid_(Great_Britain)" title="National Grid (Great Britain)">National Grid</a> was planned to be made at an electrical substation near <a href="/wiki/Salle,_Norfolk" title="Salle, Norfolk">Salle, Norfolk</a> via a 132kV 21.3 kilometres (13.2 mi) underground cable.</p>
<p>Planning consent for the wind farm was given on 8 August 2008.</p>
<p>In 2009 <a href="/wiki/Statkraft" title="Statkraft">Statkraft</a> acquired 50% of the shareholding in Scira becoming joint (50:50) owners of the development. In 2014 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_Investment_Bank" title="Green Investment Bank">Green Investment Bank</a> took a £240 million 20% stake in the wind farm.</p>
<p>The lease on the sea bed given by the Crown Estate has a duration of 50 years, and the (initial) wind farm has an estimated lifespan of 20 years. The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Energy_Act&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="2004 Energy Act (page does not exist)">2004 Energy Act</a> requires the developer to decommission the wind farm once out of use - the decommissioning plan submitted by the developer included: removal of wind turbines, cutting of foundations at seabed level or lower, whilst interarray cables would be left on the sea bed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction">Construction</span></h3>

<p>In April 2008 <a href="/wiki/Nexans" title="Nexans">Nexans</a> was awarded a €24m contract for the supply and construction of the 145kV export cables, and was awarded a €12m ($15.8m) contract in April 2009 to supply 81 km of 36kV inter-array cables. <a href="/wiki/Areva" title="Areva">Areva</a> obtained a €50 million contract to supply offshore and onshore substations in late 2008, design and project management of the offshore substations was sub-contracted to <a href="/wiki/Wood_Group" title="Wood Group">Wood Group</a>.</p>
<p>Design work on the turbine and substation foundations was contracted to <a href="/wiki/Ramboll" title="Ramboll">Ramboll</a>. The two foundations for the substations and foundations for 88 turbines were supplied by <a href="/wiki/MT_H%C3%B8jgaard" title="MT Højgaard">MT Højgaard</a>. Manufacture of the turbine monopiles and transition pieces was subcontracted to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sif_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sif Group (page does not exist)">Sif Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/Smulders" title="Smulders">Smulders</a>. The substation superstructure and foundations were subcontracted to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heerema" title="Heerema">Heerema</a> (topside), <a href="/wiki/Bladt_Industries" title="Bladt Industries">Bladt Industries</a> (transition pieces) and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sif_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sif Group (page does not exist)">Sif Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/Smulders" title="Smulders">Smulders</a> (piles). In 2009 <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens</a> obtained a €450 million contract to supply 88 wind turbines to the wind farm.</p>
<p>Onshore work began in 2009, and construction of the offshore part began March 2010. Turbine foundations installation begain in June 2010; MT Højgaard installed the substation piles and 17 of the turbine foundations, using the vessel <i>Svanen</i>, halting installation due to a previously scheduled contract for the installation vessel. The foundations installed by MT Højgaard used a special method: the support of the foundation on the delivery barge had its motion steadied by a 135t sliding support with <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_motor" title="Hydraulic motor">hydraulic motors</a> compensating for wave oscillations. Delays of several months of the arrival of foundation transport vessels, coupled with wave conditions limiting the operational window of use of the transport vessels, meant that the vessel Svanen was not able to install all the foundations. The delay had an estimated additional cost of 600 million NOK.</p>
<p>Export cable installation began late 2010, under contract to Visser &amp; Smith Marine Contracting. Work was carried out by the ships <i>Atlantic Guardian</i> (route clearance), <i>Team Oman</i> (laying), and <i>VOS Sympathy</i> (trenching).</p>
<p>The offshore substations were installed in May 2011 by Heerema. The remaining pile foundations, and the transition pieces and offshore substation topsides where installed by Seaway Heavy Lifting between May and Aug 2011 using the newly built <i>Oleg Strashnov</i>. A previous contract with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Master_Marine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Master Marine (page does not exist)">Master Marine</a> for the substation installation was cancelled and transferred to Seaway due to the delays. Foundation and transition piece installation was complete by Aug 2011.</p>
<p>Inter-array cables were laid by <i>Team Oman</i>, and installed under the sea bed by water jetting by <i>Toisa Warrior</i>. Installation was complete by Mar 2012.</p>
<p>The turbine installation was contracted to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gulf_Marine_Services&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gulf Marine Services (page does not exist)">Gulf Marine Services</a> in Dec 2010, the initial contract with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Master_Marine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Master Marine (page does not exist)">Master Marine</a> was terminated by mutual agreement after the foundation installation delays. Turbine installation was carried out by the <a href="/wiki/Jackup_rig" title="Jackup rig">jackup vessels</a> <i>Sea Jack</i>and <i>GMS Endeavour</i>, with SEA JACK replaced by <i>Sea Jack Leviathan</i> in late 2011. The first turbine was installed on 3 July 2011, and began generating in Aug 2011; installation was complete by July 2012.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations_bases">Operations bases</span></h4>
<p>As part of the development the harbour and channel at <a href="/wiki/Wells_Harbour" title="Wells Harbour">Wells Harbour</a> was dredged to allow wind farm maintenance vessels to be based there. A 150 metres (490 ft) pontoon jetty was also built; the work was completed early 2010. An onshore operations base was constructed at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Egmere&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Egmere (page does not exist)">Egmere</a>, 3 miles south of <a href="/wiki/Wells-next-the-Sea" title="Wells-next-the-Sea">Wells-next-the-Sea</a>, with building work taking place in 2012.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Operation">Operation</span></h3>
<p>The wind farm was operational by late 2011. An official opening took place on September 27, 2012 attended by Norway's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crown_Prince_Haakon" title="Crown Prince Haakon">Crown Prince Haakon</a> and UK Secretary of State <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edward_Davey" title="Edward Davey">Edward Davey</a>. The cost of development has been estimated at 10billion Norwegian krona ($1.8 billion). <a href="/wiki/Wells_Harbour" title="Wells Harbour">Wells Harbour</a> is used as the operational base for the wind farm.</p>
<p>In June 2013 a consortium, "Blue Transmission" (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Macquarie_Capital_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Macquarie Capital Group (page does not exist)">Macquarie Capital Group</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Barclays_Infrastructure_Funds&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Barclays Infrastructure Funds (page does not exist)">Barclays Infrastructure Funds</a>) acquired the electrical transmission assets of the wind farm for £193 million. In Aug 2013 <a href="/wiki/Statkraft" title="Statkraft">Statkraft</a> took over operations and maintenance of the windfarm from Scira. Statoil takes over operation from Statkraft, beginning in 2017, while also increasing its share in the proposed <a href="/wiki/Dogger_Bank_Wind_Farm" title="Dogger Bank Wind Farm">Dogger Bank Wind Farm</a>.</p>
<p>By Sep 2013 the wind farm had generated 1.2TWh in its first year of operation.</p>
