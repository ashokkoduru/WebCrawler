<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="hatnote" role="note">For other uses, see <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Raw_material_(disambiguation)" title="Raw material (disambiguation)">Raw material (disambiguation)</a>.</div>


<p>A <b>raw material</b>, also known as a <b>feedstock</b> or most correctly <b>unprocessed material</b>, is a basic material that is used to produce goods, finished products, energy, or intermediate materials which are feedstock for future finished products. As feedstock, the term connotes these materials are bottleneck assets and are highly important with regards to producing other products. An example of this is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crude_oil" title="Crude oil">crude oil</a>, which is a raw material and a feedstock used in the production of industrial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemical" title="Chemical">chemicals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fuel" title="Fuel">fuels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plastic" title="Plastic">plastics</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical" title="Pharmaceutical">pharmaceutical</a> goods; <a href="/wiki/Lumber" title="Lumber">lumber</a> is a raw material used to produce a variety of products including furniture.</p>
<p>The term "raw material" denotes materials in minimally processed or unprocessed in states; e.g., raw <a href="/wiki/Latex" title="Latex">latex</a>, crude oil, cotton, <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>, raw <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">biomass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iron_ore" title="Iron ore">iron ore</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air" title="Air">air</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lumber" title="Lumber">logs</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Seawater" title="Seawater">seawater</a> i.e. "<i>...any product of agriculture, forestry, fishing and any other mineral that is in its natural form or which has undergone the transformation required to prepare it for internationally marketing in substantial volumes.</i>"</p>
<p>Africa has 30% of global reserves of non-energy mineral raw materials but this wealth has had a negative impact upon African countries. This phenomenon, known as "<a href="/wiki/Dutch_disease" title="Dutch disease">Dutch disease</a>" or the "<a href="/wiki/Resource_curse" title="Resource curse">resource curse</a>", occurs when the economy of a country is mainly based upon its exports due to its method of governance. An example of this is the "geological scandal" of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of Congo">Democratic Republic of Congo</a> as it is rich in raw materials; the <a href="/wiki/Second_Congo_War" title="Second Congo War">Second Congo War</a> focused on controlling these raw materials.</p>
<p>Raw materials are also used by non-humans, such as birds using <a href="/wiki/Found_object" title="Found object">found objects</a> and <a href="/wiki/Twig" title="Twig">twigs</a> to create <a href="/wiki/Nest" title="Nest">nests</a>.</p>
