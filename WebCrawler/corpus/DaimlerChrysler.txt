<p><b>Daimler AG</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈdaɪmlɐ aːˈɡeː]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:Daimler_AG.ogg" title="File:Daimler AG.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/69/Daimler_AG.ogg" title="Daimler AG.ogg">listen</a>)</small>) is a German <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> automotive corporation. Daimler AG is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. As of 2014, Daimler owns or has shares in a number of car, bus, truck and motorcycle brands including <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-AMG" title="Mercedes-AMG">Mercedes-AMG</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smart_(automobile)" title="Smart (automobile)">Smart Automobile</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freightliner_LLC" title="Freightliner LLC">Freightliner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Western_Star_Trucks" title="Western Star Trucks">Western Star</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Built_Buses" title="Thomas Built Buses">Thomas Built Buses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Setra" title="Setra">Setra</a>, <a href="/wiki/BharatBenz" title="BharatBenz">BharatBenz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Fuso_Truck_and_Bus_Corporation" title="Mitsubishi Fuso Truck and Bus Corporation">Mitsubishi Fuso</a>, <a href="/wiki/MV_Agusta" title="MV Agusta">MV Agusta</a> as well as shares in <a href="/wiki/Denza" title="Denza">Denza</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KAMAZ" title="KAMAZ">KAMAZ</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beijing_Automotive_Group" title="Beijing Automotive Group">Beijing Automotive Group</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Maybach" title="Maybach">Maybach</a> brand was closed at the end of 2012, but was revived in April 2015 as "<a href="/wiki/Maybach#Mercedes-Maybach" title="Maybach">Mercedes-Maybach</a>", an ultra luxury edition of the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class">Mercedes-Benz S-Class</a>. In 2015 Daimler sold 2.9 million vehicles. By unit sales, Daimler is the thirteenth-largest <a href="/wiki/Automotive_industry" title="Automotive industry">car manufacturer</a> and second-largest truck manufacturer in the world. In addition to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobiles" title="Automobiles">automobiles</a>, Daimler manufactures buses and provides financial services through its <a href="/wiki/Daimler_Financial_Services" title="Daimler Financial Services">Daimler Financial Services</a> arm. The company is a component of the <a href="/wiki/Euro_Stoxx_50" title="Euro Stoxx 50">Euro Stoxx 50</a> <a href="/wiki/Stock_market_index" title="Stock market index">stock market index</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p>Daimler AG is a German manufacturer of automobiles, motor vehicles, and engines, which dates back more than a century.</p>
<p>An <i>Agreement of Mutual Interest</i> was signed on 1 May 1924 between <a href="/wiki/Karl_Benz#Benz_.26_Cie._and_the_Benz_Patent_Motorwagen" title="Karl Benz">Benz &amp; Cie</a> (founded 1883 by <a href="/wiki/Karl_Benz" title="Karl Benz">Karl Benz</a>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimler_Motoren_Gesellschaft" title="Daimler Motoren Gesellschaft">Daimler Motoren Gesellschaft</a> (founded 1890 by <a href="/wiki/Gottlieb_Daimler" title="Gottlieb Daimler">Gottlieb Daimler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Maybach" title="Wilhelm Maybach">Wilhelm Maybach</a>).</p>
<p>Both companies continued to manufacture their separate automobile and internal combustion engine brands until, on 28 June 1926, when Benz &amp; Cie. and Daimler Motoren Gesellschaft AG formally merged—becoming <a href="/wiki/Daimler-Benz" title="Daimler-Benz">Daimler-Benz</a> <a href="/wiki/Aktiengesellschaft" title="Aktiengesellschaft">AG</a>—and agreed that, thereafter, all of the factories would use the brand name of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a> on their automobiles.</p>
<p>In 1998, Daimler-Benz and Chrysler Corporation announced the world's largest cross-border deal ever, valued at <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$</span>38billion, and the resulting change in company name to "DaimlerChrysler AG".</p>
<p>In 2007, when the Chrysler group was sold off to <a href="/wiki/Cerberus_Capital_Management" title="Cerberus Capital Management">Cerberus Capital Management</a> (see below), the name of the parent company was changed to simply "Daimler AG".</p>
<p>In November 2014, Daimler announced it would acquire 25 percent of Italian motorcycle producer <a href="/wiki/MV_Agusta" title="MV Agusta">MV Agusta</a> for an undisclosed fee.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger_with_Chrysler">Merger with Chrysler</span></h3>
<p>Main Article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DaimlerChrysler_Corporation_AG" title="DaimlerChrysler Corporation AG">DaimlerChrysler Corporation AG</a></p>

<p>In a so-called "Merger of Equals," or "Marriage made in Heaven", according to its then CEO and architect <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_E._Schrempp" title="Jürgen E. Schrempp">Jürgen E. Schrempp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daimler-Benz" title="Daimler-Benz">Daimler-Benz</a> AG and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>-based automobile manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler Corporation</a>, the smallest of the three American automakers, merged in 1998 in an exchange of shares as Daimler-Benz AG bought 92% of Chrysler, and 8% of Chrysler remained independent and formed DaimlerChrysler AG. The terms of the merger allowed <a href="/wiki/Daimler-Benz" title="Daimler-Benz">Daimler-Benz</a>'s non-automotive businesses such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimler-Benz_InterServices_AG_(Debis)" title="Daimler-Benz InterServices AG (Debis)">Daimler-Benz InterServices AG</a>, "debis AG" for short, (created in 1989 to handle data processing, financial and insurance services, and real estate management for the Daimler group) to continue to pursue their respective strategies of expansion. <i>debis AG</i> reported revenues of $8.6 bn (DM 15.5 bn) in 1997.</p>
<p>The merger was contentious with investors launching lawsuits over whether the transaction was the 'merger of equals' that senior management claimed or actually amounted to a Daimler-Benz takeover of Chrysler. A <a href="/wiki/Class_action" title="Class action">class action</a> investor lawsuit was settled in August 2003 for US$300 million while a suit by billionaire investor activist <a href="/wiki/Kirk_Kerkorian" title="Kirk Kerkorian">Kirk Kerkorian</a> was dismissed on 7 April 2005. The transaction claimed the job of its architect, Chairman Jürgen E. Schrempp, who resigned at the end of 2005 in response to the fall of the company's share price following the transaction. The merger was also the subject of a book <i>Taken for a Ride: How Daimler-Benz Drove Off With Chrysler</i>, (2000) by Bill Vlasic and Bradley A. Stertz.</p>
<p>Another issue of contention is whether the merger delivered promised synergies and successfully integrated the two businesses. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_H._Wiggers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Martin H. Wiggers (page does not exist)">Martin H. Wiggers</a>' concept of a platform strategy like the VW Group, was implemented only for a few models, so the synergy effects in development and production were too low. As late as 2002, DaimlerChrysler appeared to run two independent product lines. Later that year, the company launched products that appeared to integrate elements from both sides of the company, including the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Crossfire" title="Chrysler Crossfire">Chrysler Crossfire</a>, which was based on the Mercedes SLK platform and utilized Mercedes's 3.2L V6, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dodge_Sprinter" title="Dodge Sprinter">Dodge Sprinter</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freightliner_Sprinter" title="Freightliner Sprinter">Freightliner Sprinter</a>, a re-badged <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_Sprinter" title="Mercedes-Benz Sprinter">Mercedes-Benz Sprinter</a> van. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DaimlerChrysler_Corporation_AG" title="DaimlerChrysler Corporation AG">DaimlerChrysler Corporation AG</a></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sale_of_Chrysler">Sale of Chrysler</span></h4>
<p>Daimler agreed to sell the Chrysler unit to <a href="/wiki/Cerberus_Capital_Management" title="Cerberus Capital Management">Cerberus Capital Management</a> in May 2007 for US$6 billion. Through most of its history, Chrysler has been the smallest of the "Big 3" U.S. automakers, but in January 2007, DaimlerChrysler, excluding its luxury Mercedes and Maybach lines, also outsold traditionally second place Ford, though behind General Motors and Toyota.</p>
<p>Chrysler reported losses of US$1.5 billion in 2006. It then announced plans to lay off 13,000 employees in mid-February 2007, close a major assembly plant and reduce production at other plants in order to restore profitability by 2008.</p>
<p>DaimlerChrysler had reportedly approached other carmakers and investment groups to sell Chrysler in early 2007. <a href="/wiki/General_Motors" title="General Motors">General Motors</a> was reported to be a suitor, but on 3 August 2007, DaimlerChrysler completed the sale of Chrysler Group to <a href="/wiki/Cerberus_Capital_Management" title="Cerberus Capital Management">Cerberus Capital Management</a>. The original agreement stated that Cerberus would take an 80.1 percent stake in the new company, Chrysler Holding LLC. DaimlerChrysler changed its name to Daimler AG and retained the remaining 19.9% stake in the separated Chrysler.</p>
<p>The terms saw Daimler pay Cerberus US$650 million to take Chrysler and associated liabilities off its hands. Of the US$7.4 billion purchase price, <a href="/wiki/Cerberus_Capital_Management" title="Cerberus Capital Management">Cerberus Capital Management</a> was to invest US$5 billion in Chrysler Holdings and US$1.05 billion in Chrysler's financial unit. The de-merged Daimler AG received US$1.35 billion directly from Cerberus but directly invested US$2 billion in Chrysler itself.</p>
<p>Since Chrysler's <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Chapter_11_reorganization" title="Chrysler Chapter 11 reorganization">2009 bankruptcy filing in the United States</a>, Chrysler has been controlled by Italian automaker <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fiat" title="Fiat">Fiat</a> and is (as of 2016) still working to integrate Chrysler's products into the Fiat portfolio, such as <a href="/wiki/Lancia" title="Lancia">Lancia</a> and Chrysler's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chrysler_(division)" title="Chrysler (division)">namesake brand</a>, and Fiat's <a href="/wiki/Fiat_Automobiles" title="Fiat Automobiles">namesake brand</a> with <a href="/wiki/Dodge" title="Dodge">Dodge</a>. Interestingly despite the fact it had been nearly a decade since the Daimler/Chrysler split, the fourth-generation <a href="/wiki/Jeep_Grand_Cherokee" title="Jeep Grand Cherokee">Jeep Grand Cherokee</a> shares a platform with the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_M-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz M-Class">Mercedes-Benz M-Class</a>. This also includes the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_LX_platform" title="Chrysler LX platform">Chrysler LX platform</a> vehicles which initially used Mercedes-Benz components since its 2005 introduction.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Automated_cars">Automated cars</span></h3>
<p>On 3 August 2015, <a href="/wiki/Nokia" title="Nokia">Nokia</a> announced that it had reached a deal to sell its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Here_(Nokia)" title="Here (Nokia)">Here</a> digital maps division to a consortium of three German automakers—<a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>, Daimler AG, and <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Group" title="Volkswagen Group">Volkswagen Group</a>, for €2.8 billion. This was seen as an indication that the automakers were interested in automated cars.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_Charleston_Expansion">North Charleston Expansion</span></h3>
<p>On 5 March 2015, Daimler AG announced a 1,200 jobs package to the North Charleston region for its van plant. This will allow the company to start manufacturing Mercedes-Benz Sprinter vans from scratch in a North Charleston plant to meet demand in North America. Currently, these vans are set up in Germany, then shipped to the United States partially disassembled for reassembly. This is all to avoid import tariffs, a practice that started in 2010. A Daimler official said that the Sprinter’s popularity in North America is making that process less efficient. The North Charleston plant had been employing only 100 workers. The Sprinter is available on the U.S. market as a panel van, crew bus and chassis in several variants with three lengths and roof heights, six-cylinder diesel or gasoline engines. The Sprinter has been assembled and sold in the United States since 2001.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Joint_ventures_and_alliances">Joint ventures and alliances</span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Beijing_Automotive_Group">Beijing Automotive Group</span></h4>
<p>In February 2013, Daimler acquired a 12% stake in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beijing_Automotive_Group" title="Beijing Automotive Group">Beijing Automotive Industry Holding Co Ltd</a> (BAIC), becoming the first western car manufacturer to own a stake in a Chinese company.</p>
<p>Daimler works with China's <a href="/wiki/Foton_Motor" title="Foton Motor">Beiqi Foton</a> (a subsidiary of BAIC) to build Auman trucks.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Denza">Denza</span></h4>
<p>In 2010 <a href="/wiki/BYD_Auto" title="BYD Auto">BYD Auto</a> and Daimler AG created a new joint venture Shenzhen BYD Daimler New Technology Co., Ltd. In 2012 the new brand Denza was launched by the joint venture to specialise in electric cars.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Fujian_Benz">Fujian Benz</span></h4>
<p>In 2007 Daimler created a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Fujian_Motors_Group" title="Fujian Motors Group">Fujian Motors Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/China_Motor_Corporation" title="China Motor Corporation">China Motor Corporation</a> and created <a href="/wiki/Fujian_Benz" title="Fujian Benz">Fujian Benz</a> (originally Fujian Daimler Automotive Co.).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Renault-Nissan_and_Daimler_Alliance">Renault-Nissan and Daimler Alliance</span></h4>
<p>On 7 April 2010 <a href="/wiki/Renault" title="Renault">Renault</a>-<a href="/wiki/Nissan" title="Nissan">Nissan</a> executives, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Ghosn" title="Carlos Ghosn">Carlos Ghosn</a> and Dr. <a href="/wiki/Dieter_Zetsche" title="Dieter Zetsche">Dieter Zetsche</a> announced a partnership between the three companies. The first fruits of the alliance in 2012 included engine sharing (<a href="/wiki/Infiniti_Q50" title="Infiniti Q50">Infiniti Q50</a> utilising Mercedes diesel engines) and a re-badged Renault Kangoo being sold as a Mercedes-Benz Citan.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_propulsion">Alternative propulsion</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Biofuel_research">Biofuel research</span></h3>
<p>Daimler AG is involved in a joint project with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archer_Daniels_Midland_Company" title="Archer Daniels Midland Company">Archer Daniels Midland Company</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayer_CropScience" title="Bayer CropScience">Bayer CropScience</a> to develop the semi-evergreen shrub <a href="/wiki/Jatropha_curcas" title="Jatropha curcas">jatropha curcas</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Biofuel" title="Biofuel">biofuel</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Electric">Electric</span></h3>
<p>Daimler AG and the utility company <a href="/wiki/RWE" title="RWE">RWE AG</a> were set in 2009 to begin a joint electric car and <a href="/wiki/Charging_station" title="Charging station">charging station</a> test project in the German capital, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, called "E-Mobility Berlin".</p>
<p>Following trials in 2007 and then with <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Motors" title="Tesla Motors">Tesla</a> in 2009, Daimler is building a production <a href="/wiki/Smart_electric_drive" title="Smart electric drive">Smart electric drive</a> car using Tesla's battery technology. Daimler temporarily invested in Tesla, saving it from bankruptcy. Daimler's joint venture with BYD has resulted in the creation of the new brand <a href="/wiki/Denza" title="Denza">Denza</a>.</p>
<p>In 2016 Daimler subsidiary ACCUMOTIVE announced their stationary batteries, to store up to 20 kWh of <a href="/wiki/Solar_power" title="Solar power">solar power</a> for later use. Daimler plans to invest €1.5 billion in battery technology.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fuel_cell">Fuel cell</span></h3>
<p>Daimler has been involved with <a href="/wiki/Fuel_cell_vehicle" title="Fuel cell vehicle">fuel cell vehicle</a> development for some time, with a number of research and concept vehicles shown and demonstrated, the first being the 2002 <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_F-Cell" title="Mercedes-Benz F-Cell">Mercedes-Benz F-Cell</a> car and the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_Citaro" title="Mercedes-Benz Citaro">Mercedes-Benz Citaro</a> Hydrogen bus. In 2013, the Renault-Nissan/Daimler alliance was joined by Ford to further develop the fuel cell technology with an aim for production by 2017.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hybrid">Hybrid</span></h3>
<p>Mercedes-Benz launched its first passenger car model equipped with a hybrid drive system in summer 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class_(W221)#S_400_Hybrid_.282009.E2.80.932013.29" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class (W221)">Mercedes-Benz S-Class 400 Hybrid</a>. and the Citaro Hybrid bus in 2007. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimler_Trucks" title="Daimler Trucks">Daimler Trucks</a> and Mitusbishi Fuso have also trailed various hybrid models including the <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Fuso_Canter_Eco_Hybrid" title="Mitsubishi Fuso Canter Eco Hybrid">Mitsubishi Fuso Canter Eco Hybrid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Fuso_Aero_Star" title="Mitsubishi Fuso Aero Star">Mitsubishi Fuso Aero Star</a> Aero Star Eco Hybrid bus.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formula_One">Formula One</span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes-Benz in Formula One</a></div>
<p>On 16 November 2009 Daimler (45.1%) och <a href="/wiki/Aabar_Investments" title="Aabar Investments">Aabar Investments</a> (30%) purchased a 75.1% stake in <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a>. The company was rebranded as <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes GP</a> with its base in <a href="/wiki/Brackley" title="Brackley">Brackley</a>, UK, with <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a> remaining team principal., However the purchase of Brawn meant that Daimler sold back its stake in <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> in stages that ended in 2011. Mercedes continued to provide sponsorship and engines to McLaren until 2015, after which McLaren switched to engines from <a href="/wiki/Honda#Automobile" title="Honda">Honda</a>.</p>
<p>Prior to the 2011 season, Daimler and Aabar Investments purchased the remaining 24.9% stake owned by the team management in February 2011. In November 2012 Aabar Investments sold its remaining shares and the team (rebranded as Mercedes <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-AMG" title="Mercedes-AMG">AMG</a> <a href="/wiki/Petronas" title="Petronas">Petronas</a> F1 Team) is now wholly owned by Daimler.</p>
<p>Daimler also owns <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains</a>, which as of 2014 supplies engines to <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, McLaren and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, in addition to Mercedes AMG Petronas.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bribery_and_corruption">Bribery and corruption</span></h2>
<p>On 1 April 2010, Daimler AG's German and Russian subsidiaries each plead guilty to two counts of bribery charges brought by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Justice_Department" title="U.S. Justice Department">U.S. Justice Department</a> and the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a>. Daimler itself had to pay US$185 million as a <a href="/wiki/Settlement_(litigation)" title="Settlement (litigation)">settlement</a>, but the company and its Chinese subsidiary remained subject to a two-year <a href="/wiki/Deferred_prosecution" title="Deferred prosecution">deferred prosecution</a> <a href="/wiki/Contract" title="Contract">agreement</a> requiring further cooperation with regulators, adherence to internal controls and meeting other terms before final sentencing. Daimler would face harsher penalties should it fail to meet the terms of the agreement during the two-year period.</p>
<p>Additionally, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_J._Freeh" title="Louis J. Freeh">Louis J. Freeh</a>, a former director of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Bureau_of_Investigation" title="Federal Bureau of Investigation">Federal Bureau of Investigation</a>, served as an independent monitor to oversee Daimler's compliance with anti-bribery laws.</p>
<p>U.S. prosecutors accused key executives of Daimler, Daimler <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subsidiaries" title="Subsidiaries">subsidiaries</a>, and Daimler <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Affiliates" title="Affiliates">affiliates</a> of illegally showering foreign officials with money and gifts between 1998 and 2008 to secure government contracts around the world. The investigation for the case revealed that Daimler improperly paid some $56 million in bribes related to more than 200 transactions in at least 22 countries (including China, Russia, Turkey, Hungary, Greece, Latvia, Serbia and Montenegro, Egypt and Nigeria, among other places) that, in return, awarded the company $1.9 billion in revenue and at least $91.4 million in illegal profits.</p>
<p>The SEC case was sparked in 2004 after David Bazzetta, a former auditor at then DaimlerChrysler Corp, filed a <a href="/wiki/Whistleblower" title="Whistleblower">whistleblower</a> complaint after he was fired for raising questions about bank accounts controlled by Mercedes-Benz units in South America. Bazzetta alleged that he learned in a July 2001 corporate audit executive committee meeting in <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a> that business units "continued to maintain secret bank accounts to bribe foreign government officials", though the company knew the practice violated U.S. laws.</p>
<p>In another attempt to silence Bazzetta, Daimler later offered to settle his termination of employment suit out of court and he eventually accepted a settlement. But Daimler's strategy with Bazzetta proved to be a failure as the U.S. criminal investigation for violating anti-bribery laws was already underway in what has been one of the most wide-ranging cases brought against a foreign corporation.</p>
<p>According to the charges, the bribes were frequently made by over-invoicing customers and paying the excess back to top government officials or their proxies. The bribes also took the form of luxury European vacations, armored Mercedes vehicles for high-ranking government officials and a birthday gift to the then notorious dictator of <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>, Turkmenbashi (<a href="/wiki/Saparmurat_Niyazov" title="Saparmurat Niyazov">Saparmurat Niyazov</a>), including a golden box and 10,000 copies of his personal <a href="/wiki/Manifesto" title="Manifesto">manifesto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ruhnama" title="Ruhnama">Ruhnama</a>, translated into German.</p>
<p>Investigators also found that the firm violated the terms of the United Nations' <a href="/wiki/Oil-for-Food_Programme#Daimler_AG_Kickbacks_Case" title="Oil-for-Food Programme">Oil-for-Food Programme</a> with <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> by giving <a href="/wiki/Kickback_(bribery)" title="Kickback (bribery)">kickbacks</a> worth 10% of the contract values to officials within the Iraqi government, then led by <a href="/wiki/Saddam_Hussein" title="Saddam Hussein">Saddam Hussein</a>. The SEC said the company made more than $4 million in profit from the sale of vehicles and spare parts in the corrupt Oil-for-Food deals.</p>
<p>U.S. prosecutors further alleged that some bribes were paid through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shell_companies" title="Shell companies">shell companies</a> based in the U.S. "In some cases Daimler wired these improper payments to U.S. bank accounts or to the foreign bank accounts of U.S. shell companies in order to transmit the bribe," the court papers said.</p>
<p>Prosecutors said that Daimler engaged in a "long-standing practice" of paying bribes, due in part to a corporate culture that encouraged the practice.</p>
<p>"Using <a href="/wiki/Offshore_bank" title="Offshore bank">offshore bank</a> accounts, third-party agents and deceptive pricing practices, these companies [Daimler AG, its subsidiaries and affiliates] saw foreign bribery as a way of doing business," said <a href="/wiki/Mythili_Raman" title="Mythili Raman">Mythili Raman</a>, a principal deputy in the Justice Department's criminal division.</p>
<p>"It is no exaggeration to describe corruption and bribe-paying at Daimler as a standard business practice," <a href="/wiki/Robert_Khuzami" title="Robert Khuzami">Robert Khuzami</a>, director of the SEC's enforcement division, said in a statement.</p>
<p>"We have learned a lot from past experience," Dieter Zetsche, chairman of Daimler's board, said in a statement.</p>
<p>As per the agreement with prosecutors, the two Daimler subsidiaries admitted to knowingly violating the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Corrupt_Practices_Act" title="Foreign Corrupt Practices Act">Foreign Corrupt Practices Act</a>, which bars companies and their officials from paying bribes to foreign officials to win business. The Foreign Corrupt Practices Act applies to any company that lists its shares on U.S. <a href="/wiki/Stock_exchange" title="Stock exchange">stock exchanges</a>. Daimler AG was listed with the symbol "DAI" on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>, giving the Justice Department jurisdiction over the German car maker's payments in countries around the globe.</p>
<p>Judge <a href="/wiki/Richard_J._Leon" title="Richard J. Leon">Richard J. Leon</a> of the United States District Court in Washington, D.C., approved the plea agreement and settlement, calling it a "just resolution."</p>
<p>The primary case is <i>USA v. Daimler AG,</i> <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_District_of_Columbia" title="United States District Court for the District of Columbia">United States District Court for the District of Columbia</a>, No. 1:10-cr-00063-RJL .</p>
