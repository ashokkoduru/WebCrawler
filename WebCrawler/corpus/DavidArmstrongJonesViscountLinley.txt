<p><b>David Albert Charles Armstrong-Jones, Viscount Linley</b> (born 3 November 1961), known professionally as <b>David Linley</b>, is an English furniture maker and chairman of the auction house <a href="/wiki/Christie%27s" title="Christie's">Christie's</a> UK. The son of <a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaret,_Countess_of_Snowdon" title="Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon">Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Antony_Armstrong-Jones,_1st_Earl_of_Snowdon" title="Antony Armstrong-Jones, 1st Earl of Snowdon">Antony Armstrong-Jones, 1st Earl of Snowdon</a>, he is a grandson of King <a href="/wiki/George_VI" title="George VI">George VI</a>, is 18th in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Line_of_succession_to_the_British_throne" title="Line of succession to the British throne">line to succeed</a> <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a>, his maternal aunt, and is the heir-apparent to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earldom_of_Snowdon" title="Earldom of Snowdon">Earldom of Snowdon</a>.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span></h2>
<p>At the age of five, Linley started lessons in the Buckingham Palace schoolroom with his cousin, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Andrew,_Duke_of_York" title="Prince Andrew, Duke of York">Prince Andrew</a>. He went to several <a href="/wiki/Independent_school" title="Independent school">independent schools</a>: first, to Gibbs Pre-Preparatory School in <a href="/wiki/Kensington" title="Kensington">Kensington</a> in London, followed by the pre-preparatory section of <a href="/wiki/Ashdown_House,_East_Sussex" title="Ashdown House, East Sussex">Ashdown House School</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Sussex" title="East Sussex">East Sussex</a>, then on to <a class="external text" href="http://www.education.gov.uk/edubase/establishment/summary.xhtml?urn=123305" rel="nofollow">Millbrook House School</a>, near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abingdon,_Oxfordshire" title="Abingdon, Oxfordshire">Abingdon</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a>, and finally to <a href="/wiki/Bedales_School" title="Bedales School">Bedales School</a>, where he developed a passion for arts and crafts. From 1980 to 1982, he studied at Parnham College in the small town of <a href="/wiki/Beaminster" title="Beaminster">Beaminster</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>, for craftsmen in wood.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_life">Professional life</span></h2>

<p>Linley opened a workshop in <a href="/wiki/Dorking" title="Dorking">Dorking</a> where he designed and made furniture for three years before setting up his own company David Linley Furniture Ltd, now known as LINLEY, where he makes <a href="/wiki/Bespoke" title="Bespoke">bespoke</a> furniture, upholstery, and interior design products known for their neoclassical appearance and use of inlaid woods. He has written numerous books and lectured around the world. His work is sold in retail stores in <a href="/wiki/Belgravia" title="Belgravia">Belgravia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harrods" title="Harrods">Harrods</a> and overseas including the <a href="/wiki/Bespoke_Collection" title="Bespoke Collection">Bespoke Collection</a>.</p>
<p>On 1 December 2006, Linley took up the post of chairman of <a href="/wiki/Christie%27s" title="Christie's">Christie's</a> UK, having joined the board in 2005 as a non-executive director.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_family">Personal life and family</span></h2>
<p>On 8 April 2002, Linley, along with <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">the Prince of Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Andrew,_Duke_of_York" title="Prince Andrew, Duke of York">the Duke of York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward,_Earl_of_Wessex" title="Prince Edward, Earl of Wessex">the Earl of Wessex</a>, stood guard at the lying-in-state of their grandmother, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_The_Queen_Mother" title="Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother">Queen Elizabeth, The Queen Mother</a>. This was a mark of respect unofficially known as the <a href="/wiki/Vigil_of_the_Princes" title="Vigil of the Princes">Vigil of the Princes</a>, which had taken place only once before, during the lying-in-state of <a href="/wiki/George_V" title="George V">King George V</a> in 1936.</p>
<p>Linley had romances with <a href="/wiki/Susannah_Constantine" title="Susannah Constantine">Susannah Constantine</a>, Kate Menzies and Nicola Formby. On 8 October 1993, he married <a href="/wiki/Serena_Armstrong-Jones,_Viscountess_Linley" title="Serena Armstrong-Jones, Viscountess Linley">the Hon. Serena Stanhope</a>, a daughter of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Stanhope,_12th_Earl_of_Harrington" title="Charles Stanhope, 12th Earl of Harrington">Viscount Petersham (later the 12th Earl of Harrington)</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Margaret%27s,_Westminster" title="St. Margaret's, Westminster">St. Margaret's Church, Westminster</a>.</p>
<p>Viscount Linley and his wife have two children:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>The Hon.</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_Armstrong-Jones" title="Charles Armstrong-Jones"><b>Charles</b> Patrick Inigo Armstrong-Jones</a> (born 1 July 1999, now aged 17)</li>
<li><i>The Hon.</i> <a href="/wiki/Margarita_Armstrong-Jones" title="Margarita Armstrong-Jones"><b>Margarita</b> Elizabeth Rose Alleyne Armstrong-Jones</a> (born 14 May 2002, now aged 14)</li>
</ul>
<p>Linley has one full sister, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Sarah_Chatto" title="Lady Sarah Chatto">Lady Sarah Chatto</a> (<i>née</i> Armstrong-Jones); and one paternal half-sister, Lady Frances von Hofmannsthal (née Armstrong-Jones). Viscount Linley also has a half-brother, Jasper Cable-Alexander, son of his father and Melanie Cable-Alexander, an editor at <a href="/wiki/Country_Life_(magazine)" title="Country Life (magazine)"><i>Country Life</i></a> magazine.</p>
<p>From 2000–2002, Linley, his wife and son lived in <a href="/wiki/Kensington_Palace" title="Kensington Palace">Kensington Palace</a> with his mother, Princess Margaret, in her declining years.</p>
<p>In 2011, Linley's daughter, <a href="/wiki/Margarita_Armstrong-Jones" title="Margarita Armstrong-Jones">Margarita Armstrong-Jones</a>, was a bridesmaid at the wedding of the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Cambridge" title="Duke of Cambridge">Duke of Cambridge</a>.</p>
<p>The Linleys have three homes: a flat in <a href="/wiki/Chelsea,_London" title="Chelsea, London">Chelsea, London</a>; a cottage on the <a href="/wiki/Daylesford,_Gloucestershire" title="Daylesford, Gloucestershire">Daylesford estate</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a>; and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chateau_d%27Autet" title="Chateau d'Autet">Chateau d'Autet</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Luberon" title="Luberon">Luberon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Provence" title="Provence">Provence</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles.2C_styles_and_arms">Titles, styles and arms</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Title">Title</span></h3>
<p>Though his mother was a princess, in the United Kingdom royal titles and styles are passed only from the father. He does not hold the title <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Snowdon" title="Earl of Snowdon">Viscount Linley</a> in his own right; rather, it is a <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_title" title="Courtesy title">courtesy title</a> used by the eldest son and <a href="/wiki/Heir_apparent" title="Heir apparent">heir apparent</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Snowdon" title="Earl of Snowdon">Earl of Snowdon</a>.</p>
<p><br/></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancestry">Ancestry</span></h2>
<p>Linley is the son of <a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaret,_Countess_of_Snowdon" title="Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon">Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Antony_Armstrong-Jones,_1st_Earl_of_Snowdon" title="Antony Armstrong-Jones, 1st Earl of Snowdon">1st Earl of Snowdon</a>; thus he is the nephew of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a>, and a grandson of <a href="/wiki/George_VI" title="George VI">George VI</a>. At the time of his birth he was fifth in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Line_of_succession_to_the_British_throne" title="Line of succession to the British throne">line of succession to the British throne</a>; he is currently in eighteenth place and is the first person in the line of succession who is not a descendant of the Queen.</p>
