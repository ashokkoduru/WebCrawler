<p><b>SDEWES Centre</b> (<b>The International Centre for Sustainable Development of Energy, Water and Environment Systems)</b>, is a non-governmental scientific organization based at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Zagreb" title="University of Zagreb">University of Zagreb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission">Mission</span></h2>
<p>SDEWES Centre is dedicated to the improvement and dissemination of knowledge on methods, policies and technologies for increasing the <a href="/wiki/Our_Common_Future" title="Our Common Future">sustainability of development</a> by <a href="/wiki/Decoupling_Natural_Resource_Use_and_Environmental_Impacts_from_Economic_Growth_report" title="Decoupling Natural Resource Use and Environmental Impacts from Economic Growth report">de-coupling growth from natural resources</a> and replacing them with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knowledge-based_economy" title="Knowledge-based economy">knowledge-based economy</a>, taking into account its economic, environmental and social pillars. One of the main issues of the coming decades is to improve efficiencies by integrating various life supporting systems, using <a href="/wiki/Waste" title="Waste">waste</a> from one, as resource in other, and in exact moment when it is beneficial to all, integrating <a href="/wiki/Power_engineering" title="Power engineering">electricity</a>, <a href="/wiki/HVAC#Heating" title="HVAC">heating</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cooling" title="Cooling">cooling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transport" title="Transport">transport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_supply_network" title="Water supply network">water</a>, <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">buildings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Industrial_ecology" title="Industrial ecology">industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> systems.</p>
<p>The Centre organizes courses, summer schools, public lectures, seminars and workshops in order to promote <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a> of <a href="/wiki/Energy_development" title="Energy development">energy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_supply_network" title="Water supply network">water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment" title="Human impact on the environment">environment</a> systems and provides professional opinion on issues of <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a> and sustainability metrics. The Centre also organizes a series of international SDEWES conferences for scientists to discuss issues of sustainability of <a href="/wiki/Energy_development" title="Energy development">energy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_supply_network" title="Water supply network">water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment" title="Human impact on the environment">environment</a> system. Through these actions, SDEWES Centre aims to provide a research platform that will provide comprehensive R&amp;D activities, assessment and consultation on the research subjects of sustainable development.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p>SDEWES started as a project co-funded by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Community_Research_%26_Development_Information_Service" title="Community Research &amp; Development Information Service">CORDIS</a> under the FP5 – INCO 2 programme in 2002, when first Dubrovnik Conference on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development" title="Sustainable Development">Sustainable Development</a> of <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">Energy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">Water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environment_(systems)" title="Environment (systems)">Environment</a> Systems was organized. Partners in this project were <a href="/wiki/University_of_Zagreb" title="University of Zagreb">University of Zagreb</a> and <a href="/wiki/Instituto_Superior_T%C3%A9cnico" title="Instituto Superior Técnico">Instituto Superior Técnico</a> (Lisbon). After the organisation of three more conferences in 2003, 2005 and 2007, SDEWES Centre was established in 2009. Since then, seven more conferences were organized in 2009, 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014, and 2015.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="SDEWES-Skopje">SDEWES-Skopje</span></h2>
<p>New section of SDEWES Centre is established in <a href="/wiki/Skopje" title="Skopje">Skopje</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Macedonia</a>, with the goal to gather professionals and scientists from broad range of disciplines, particularly located in the <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Europe" title="Southeast Europe">Southeast Europe</a>, in order to provide scientific support for wise policy-making. SDEWES-Skopje aims to extend the activities at regional level including the other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EU" title="EU">EU</a> candidate and potential candidate countries, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EU" title="EU">EU</a> neighbourhood countries to foster regional coordination in providing feasible solutions for the common challenges and gaining synergy effects.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Membership">Membership</span></h2>
<p>According to the Statute of SDEWES Centre, every person can under the same circumstances apply for membership.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conferences">Conferences</span></h2>
<p>Up to 2011, SDEWES Conference was organized as biennial conference in <a href="/wiki/Dubrovnik" title="Dubrovnik">Dubrovnik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>. From the year 2012, it is an annual event but with a geographic regime. Every two years the conference is held in Dubrovnik, and in the years between on different locations.</p>
<p>The SDEWES 2012 Conference was held in <a href="/wiki/Ohrid" title="Ohrid">Ohrid</a>, and in 2014 on a cruise ship travelling between <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>.</p>
<p>In 2014, the first regional SEE SDEWES conference was held in <a href="/wiki/Ohrid" title="Ohrid">Ohrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Macedonia</a> focused on South East Europe.</p>
<p>In 2015, the 10th Conference on SDEWES was held in <a href="/wiki/Dubrovnik" title="Dubrovnik">Dubrovnik</a>. 540 scientists from over 65 countries participated.</p>
<p>In 2016, the second regional SEE SDEWES conference was held in <a href="/wiki/Piran" title="Piran">Piran</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Slovenia" title="Republic of Slovenia">Slovenia</a>.</p>
<p>Also in 2016, the 11th SDEWES Conference was held in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>.</p>
<p>Work presented at the conferences are published in scientific journals  and among them, in the Journal of Sustainable Development of Energy, Water and Environment Systems </p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Journal">Journal</span></h2>
<p>SDEWES Centre is publishing research <a href="/wiki/Open_access" title="Open access">open access</a> Journal of Sustainable Development of Energy, Water and Environment Systems since 2013.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span></h2>
<p>SDEWES Centre forms research teams from the pool of members to participate in research projects. SDEWES Centre is involved in two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seventh_Framework_Programme" title="Seventh Framework Programme">FP7</a> projects, one <a href="/wiki/Framework_Programmes_for_Research_and_Technological_Development" title="Framework Programmes for Research and Technological Development">HORIZON 2020</a> project and one project under the <a href="/wiki/European_integration" title="European integration">European Strategy for Danube Region</a> (EUSDR) fund START.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="SDEWES_Index">SDEWES Index</span></h2>
<p>In line with the aims of the SDEWES Center, an index is also developed to benchmark the performance of cities across aspects that are related to energy, water and environment systems. With the namesake of the Center, the SDEWES Index consists of 7 dimensions, 35 indicators, and close to 20 sub-indicators. It is currently applied to 58 cities.</p>
