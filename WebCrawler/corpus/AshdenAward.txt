<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>Ashden</b> is a <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>-based charity that works in the field of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_energy" title="Sustainable energy">sustainable energy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Regional_development" title="Regional development">development</a>. Its work includes the annual Ashden Awards, advocacy and research in the field of sustainable energy, and mentoring and practical support for award winners.</p>
<p>Sarah Butler-Sloss created the awards in 2001, from the Ashden Trust, one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J_Sainsbury#Sainsbury_family" title="J Sainsbury">Sainsbury Family</a> Charitable Trusts. In 2011 the charity changed its working name to Ashden, with its full name registered with the <a href="/wiki/Charity_Commission_for_England_and_Wales" title="Charity Commission for England and Wales">Charity Commission</a> being 'Ashden, Sustainable solutions, better lives'.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="About_the_Ashden_Awards">About the Ashden Awards</span></h2>
<p>Ashden rewards and promotes local <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_energy" title="Sustainable energy">sustainable energy</a> solutions in parts of Europe and the developing world through its annual Ashden Awards. Awards are given to organisations and businesses that deliver local, sustainable energy schemes with social, economic and environmental benefits. Awards are provided across several different categories, including UK and international awards. Awards for sustainable travel schemes have been provided since 2012. To help the winners, each award includes a cash prize.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Awards_ceremony">The Awards ceremony</span></h2>
<p>The Ashden Awards ceremony is held annually at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Geographical_Society" title="Royal Geographical Society">Royal Geographical Society</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. Previous hosts include broadcasters <a href="/wiki/Emma_Freud" title="Emma Freud">Emma Freud</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Ford" title="Anna Ford">Anna Ford</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Humphrys" title="John Humphrys">John Humphrys</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Dimbleby" title="Jonathan Dimbleby">Jonathan Dimbleby</a> and environmental journalist <a href="/wiki/Mark_Lynas" title="Mark Lynas">Mark Lynas</a>.</p>
<p>Guest speakers in recent years have included <a href="/wiki/Kandeh_Yumkella" title="Kandeh Yumkella">Kandeh Yumkella</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_Charles" title="Prince Charles">Prince Charles</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_David_King" title="Sir David King">Sir David King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wangari_Maathai" title="Wangari Maathai">Wangari Maathai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al_Gore" title="Al Gore">Al Gore</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Attenborough" title="David Attenborough">David Attenborough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hilary_Benn" title="Hilary Benn">Hilary Benn</a>, (the then UK Secretary of State for International Development), <a href="/wiki/Rajendra_K._Pachauri" title="Rajendra K. Pachauri">Dr RK Pachauri</a>, Chair of the UN <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change" title="Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change">Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Cameron" title="David Cameron">David Cameron</a>, (leader of the Conservative Party) and Lord May of Oxford, (former Chief Scientific Adviser to the UK Government).</p>
<p>After presenting the prizes at the 2007 ceremony <a href="/wiki/Al_Gore" title="Al Gore">Al Gore</a> commented:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><i>"No one can attend an event like the Ashden Awards and fail to be inspired. We must find a path from an unsustainable present to a sustainable future.What impresses me most about these projects is they truly are becoming the change that is needed in the world.These Awards tell us how to illuminate this path to a sustainable future together. I hope that we can make it quickly."</i></p>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Support_programmes">Support programmes</span></h2>
<p>Ashden provides a package of support for organisations after they win their awards. It also runs 'LESS CO2', a peer-to-peer mentoring programme for schools to learn from each other about energy saving. In 2011 Ashden helped set up the Ashden India Renewable Energy Collective, made up of Ashden Award winners. The Collective works to end energy poverty in India by acting as a unified voice for the sustainable energy sector.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ashden_seminars_and_conferences">Ashden seminars and conferences</span></h2>
<p>Ashden also holds specialist seminars and conferences bringing together their award winners with practitioners, academics, and those who make or influence policy. Examples of recent seminars include one held at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_College" title="Imperial College">Imperial College</a> and another held at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DFID" title="DFID">DFID</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_winners">List of winners</span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Ashden_Award_winners" title="List of Ashden Award winners">List of Ashden Award winners</a></div>
<p>The Ashden Awards have been presented to 159 organisations, including NGOs, businesses, local governments and schools.</p>
