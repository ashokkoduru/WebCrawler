<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Environmental security</b> examines threats posed by environmental events and trends to individuals, communities or nations. It may focus on the impact of human <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">conflict</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_relations" title="International relations">international relations</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environment</a>, or on how environmental problems cross <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">state</a> borders.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General">General</span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Millennium_Project" title="United Nations Millennium Project">Millennium Project</a> assessed definitions of environmental security and created a synthesis definition:</p>
<p>Environmental security is environmental viability for life support, with three sub-elements:</p>
<ul>
<li>preventing or repairing military damage to the environment,</li>
<li>preventing or responding to environmentally caused conflicts, and</li>
<li>protecting the environment due to its inherent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moral_value" title="Moral value">moral value</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>It considers the abilities of individuals, communities or nations to cope with <a href="/wiki/Environmental_hazard" title="Environmental hazard">environmental risks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">changes</a> or conflicts, or limited <a href="/wiki/Natural_Resources" title="Natural Resources">natural resources</a>. For example, climate change can be viewed a threat to environmental security. Human activity impacts CO<sub>2</sub> emissions, impacting regional and global climatic and environmental changes and thus changes in agricultural output. This can lead to food shortages which will then cause political debate, ethnic tension, and civil unrest.</p>
<p>Environmental security is an important concept in two fields: <a href="/wiki/International_relations" title="International relations">international relations</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_development" title="International development">international development</a>.</p>
<p>Within international development, projects may aim to improve aspects of environmental security such as <a href="/wiki/Food_security" title="Food security">food security</a> or <a href="/wiki/Water_security" title="Water security">water security</a>, but also connected aspects such as <a href="/wiki/Energy_security" title="Energy security">energy security</a>, that are now recognised as Sustainable Development goals at UN level. Targets for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MDG" title="MDG">MDG</a> 7 about environmental sustainability show international priorities for environmental security. Target 7B is about the security of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fisheries" title="Fisheries">fisheries</a> on which many people depend for <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">food</a>. Fisheries are an example of a resource that cannot be contained within state borders. A conflict before the <a href="/wiki/International_Court_of_Justice" title="International Court of Justice">International Court of Justice</a> between Chile and Peru about maritime borders and their associated fisheries is a case study for environmental security.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Copenhagen_School_(international_relations)" title="The Copenhagen School (international relations)">Copenhagen School</a> defines the referent object of environmental security as the environment, or some strategic part of it.</p>
<p>Historically, the definition of <a href="/wiki/International_security" title="International security">international security</a> has varied over time. After <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, definitions typically focused on the subject of <a href="/wiki/Realpolitik" title="Realpolitik">realpolitik</a> that developed during the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> between the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>.</p>
<p>As tensions between the superpowers eased after the collapse of the Soviet Union, academic discussions of definitions of security significantly expanded to encompass a far broader range of threats to peace, including, particularly, environmental threats associated with the political implications of resource use or pollution. By the mid-1980s, this field of study was becoming known as "environmental security". Despite a wide range of semantic and academic debates over terms, it is now widely acknowledged that environmental factors play both direct and indirect roles in both political disputes and violent conflicts.</p>
<p>In the academic sphere environmental security is defined as the relationship between security concerns such as armed conflict and the natural environment. A small but rapidly developing field, it has become particularly relevant for those studying resource scarcity and conflict in the developing world. Prominent early researchers in the field include <a href="/wiki/Felix_Dodds" title="Felix Dodds">Felix Dodds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norman_Myers" title="Norman Myers">Norman Myers</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jessica_Tuchman_Mathews" title="Jessica Tuchman Mathews">Jessica Tuchman Mathews</a>, Michael Renner, Richard Ullman, Arthur Westing, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Klare" title="Michael Klare">Michael Klare</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thomas_Homer_Dixon" title="Thomas Homer Dixon">Thomas Homer Dixon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Dabelko" title="Geoffrey Dabelko">Geoffrey Dabelko</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gleick" title="Peter Gleick">Peter Gleick</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseph_Romm" title="Joseph Romm">Joseph Romm</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins_of_Environmental_Security">Origins of Environmental Security</span></h3>
<p>According to Jon Barnett, environmental security emerged as an important concept in <a href="/wiki/Security_studies" title="Security studies">security studies</a> because of some interrelated developments which started in 1960s. The first one was the increasing level of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_consciousness" title="Environmental consciousness">environmental consciousness</a> in so called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developed_countries" title="Developed countries">developed countries</a>. Various occurrences and events triggered the growth of the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_movement" title="Environmental movement">environmental movement</a> during this period of time. <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Carson" title="Rachel Carson">Rachel Carson</a>'s well-known book <a href="/wiki/Silent_Spring" title="Silent Spring">Silent Spring</a> was one of the extraordinary publication of that time and brought greater degree of environmental awareness among ordinary people by warning the dangers to all natural systems including animals and food chain from the misuse of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemical_pesticides" title="Chemical pesticides">chemical pesticides</a> such as <a href="/wiki/DDT" title="DDT">DDT</a>. Thus, Carson was not arguably one of the social revolutionary at that time who urged the public awareness of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_issue" title="Environmental issue">environmental issues</a>. Moreover, the number of largest well-known <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_non-governmental_organizations" title="Environmental non-governmental organizations">environmental non-governmental organizations</a> such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wildlife_Fund" title="World Wildlife Fund">World Wildlife Fund</a> (1961), <a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Friends of the Earth">Friends of the Earth</a> (1969), and <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a> (1971) were founded during that time.</p>
<p>The second notable development which brings the emergence of concept of environmental security was number of scholars started to criticize the traditional notion of security and mainstream security debates in their work from 1970s by emphasizing its inability to handle environmental problems at national and international security level. First commentators were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Falk" title="Richard Falk">Richard Falk</a> who published‘This Endangered Planet' (1971), and Harold and Margaret Sprout who wrote‘Toward a Politics of Planet Earth'(1971). These two commentators asserted in their book that the notion of security can no longer be centered only on military power, rather nations should collectively take measurements against common environmental problems since they pose threat to national well-being and thus international stability. These main ideas about environmental interdependence between countries and common security threat have remained key themes of environmental <a href="/wiki/Security_studies" title="Security studies">security studies</a>. However, not until Richard Ullman publishes an academic article named “Redefining Security” (1983), radical departure from the dominant security discourse haven’t happened. Ullman offered the following definition of national security threat as "an action or sequence of events that (1) threatens drastically and over a relatively brief span of time to degrade the quality of life for the inhabitants of a state, or (2) threatens significantly to narrow the range of policy choices available to the government of a state, or to private, nongovernmental entities within the state". Significant other scientists onward also linked the issue of security by focusing on the role of environmental degradation in causing violent conflict.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_change_and_national_security">Environmental change and national security</span></h2>
<p>Even though <a href="/wiki/Environmental_degradation" title="Environmental degradation">environmental degradation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> sometimes cause <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">war</a> and violent conflict within and between countries and other times not, it can weaken the national security of the state in number of profound ways. <a href="/wiki/Environmental_change" title="Environmental change">Environmental change</a> can undermine the economic prosperity which plays big role in country’s military capacity and material power. In some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developed_countries" title="Developed countries">developed countries</a>, and in most <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developing_countries" title="Developing countries">developing countries</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_resources" title="Natural resources">natural resources</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_services" title="Environmental services">environmental services</a> tend to be important factors for <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Employment_rate" title="Employment rate">employment rate</a>. Income from and employment in primary sectors such as <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a>, and from environmentally dependent services like <a href="/wiki/Torus" title="Torus">torus</a>, may all be adversely affected by <a href="/wiki/Environmental_change" title="Environmental change">environmental change</a>. If <a href="/wiki/Natural_capital" title="Natural capital">natural capital</a> base of an economy erodes, then so does the long-term capacity of its armed forces. Moreover, changes in environmental condition can exposes people to health threats, it can also undermine <a href="/wiki/Human_capital" title="Human capital">human capital</a> and its well-being which are essential factors of <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">economic development</a> and stability of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_society" title="Human society">human society</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">Climate change</a> also could, through extreme weather events, have a more direct impact on <a href="/wiki/National_security" title="National security">national security</a> by damaging critical infrastructures such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_bases" title="Military bases">military bases</a>, naval yards and training grounds, thereby severely threatening essential national defense resources.</p>
