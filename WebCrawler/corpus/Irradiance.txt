<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>In <a href="/wiki/Radiometry" title="Radiometry">radiometry</a>, <b>irradiance</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Radiant_flux" title="Radiant flux">radiant flux</a> (<a href="/wiki/Power_(physics)" title="Power (physics)">power</a>) <i>received</i> by a <i>surface</i> per unit area. The <a href="/wiki/International_System_of_Units" title="International System of Units">SI unit</a> of irradiance is the <a href="/wiki/Watt" title="Watt">watt</a> per square metre (<span class="nowrap">W/m</span>). The <a href="/wiki/Centimetre%E2%80%93gram%E2%80%93second_system_of_units" title="Centimetre–gram–second system of units">CGS unit</a> <a href="/wiki/Erg" title="Erg">erg</a> per square centimetre per second (<span class="nowrap">erg·cm·s</span>) is often used in <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomy</a>. Irradiance is often called "intensity" in branches of physics other than radiometry, but in radiometry this usage leads to confusion with <a href="/wiki/Radiant_intensity" title="Radiant intensity">radiant intensity</a>.</p>
<p><b>Spectral irradiance</b> is the irradiance of a surface per unit <a href="/wiki/Frequency" title="Frequency">frequency</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wavelength" title="Wavelength">wavelength</a>, depending on whether the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spectral_radiometric_quantity" title="Spectral radiometric quantity">spectrum</a> is taken as a function of frequency or of wavelength. The two forms have different <a href="/wiki/Dimensional_analysis" title="Dimensional analysis">dimensions</a>: spectral irradiance of a frequency spectrum is measured in watts per square metre per <a href="/wiki/Hertz" title="Hertz">hertz</a> (W·m·Hz), while spectral irradiance of a wavelength spectrum is measured in watts per square metre per metre (W·m), or more commonly watts per square metre per nanometre (<span class="nowrap">W·m·nm</span>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_energy">Solar energy</span></h2>
<p>The global irradiance on a horizontal surface on Earth consists of the direct irradiance <i>E</i><sub>e,dir</sub> and diffuse irradiance <i>E</i><sub>e,diff</sub>. On a tilted plane, there is another irradiance component, <i>E</i><sub>e,refl</sub>, which is the component that is reflected from the ground.
