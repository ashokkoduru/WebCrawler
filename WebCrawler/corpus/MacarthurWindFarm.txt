<p>The <b>Macarthur Wind Farm</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Wind_farm" title="Wind farm">wind farm</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Macarthur,_Victoria" title="Macarthur, Victoria">Macarthur, Victoria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Victoria" title="Hamilton, Victoria">Hamilton</a>, 260km west of Melbourne. It is on a 5,500 ha site which has an installed capacity of 420 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megawatt" title="Megawatt">megawatts</a> (MW). Based on the prevailing wind speeds at the site, it is estimated that the long-term average generation will be approximately 1,250 GWh per year, operating at a <a href="/wiki/Capacity_factor" title="Capacity factor">capacity factor</a> of around 35%. The actual wind speed varies year-to-year, and during FY2015 the farm produced 977.9 GWh.</p>
<p>It is the largest wind farm in the southern hemisphere generating enough power for 220,000 homes and abating 1.7 million tons of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gases</a> annually, at a capacity factor of 35%. The wind farm comprises 140 <a href="/wiki/Vestas" title="Vestas">Vestas</a> V112-3.0MW <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wind_turbines" title="Wind turbines">wind turbines</a> manufactured in Denmark.</p>
<p>The project cost about A$1 billion and was fully operational in January 2013. It was constructed by Vestas and <a href="/wiki/CIMIC_Group#CPB_Contractors" title="CIMIC Group">Leighton Contractors</a>. The first turbines were connected to the grid in September 2012. AGL also invested an additional $27m in the substation, which is completely owned by the company.</p>
<p>The project was proposed in 2004 and approved by the Victorian government in October 2006. The project was developed by a joint venture formed by <a href="/wiki/AGL_Energy" title="AGL Energy">AGL Energy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meridian_Energy" title="Meridian Energy">Meridian Energy</a>, a New Zealand-based power generating company, with works starting in 2010. In 2013, Meridian sold its 50% share in the wind farm to Malaysian power company <a href="/wiki/Malakoff_(power_company)" title="Malakoff (power company)">Malakoff Corporation Berhad</a> for A$650 million. In September 2015, AGL Energy sold its 50% stake in the wind farm to H R L Morrison &amp; Co managed funds (Morrison &amp; Co) for $532m. AGL Energy will operate and maintain the wind farm instead of Morrison &amp; Co and Malakoff. It also reserves the rights to all renewable energy certificates and electricity production until 2038.</p>
