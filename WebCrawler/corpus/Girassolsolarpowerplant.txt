<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
<p>The <b>Moura Photovoltaic Power Station</b> (also known as <b>Amareleja Photovoltaic Power Station</b>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Photovoltaic_power_station" title="Photovoltaic power station">photovoltaic power station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Amareleja" title="Amareleja">Amareleja</a>, in the municipality of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moura_Municipality" title="Moura Municipality">Moura</a>, Portugal. It is one of the largest power stations of its kind, and is built in one of the sunniest regions in Europe. Its construction involved two stages: stage 1 was completed in 2008 after 13 months, and stage 2 was completed in 2010. The entire project topped a total cost of <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>250 million.</p>
<p>Stage 2 of the project involved the construction of a further 20 MW of solar panels. It will occupy an area of 618 acres (250 ha), and will be capable of producing 93 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GWh" title="GWh">GWh</a> of electrical energy annually (10 MW average - equivalent to the electricity consumption of 15,000 Europeans).</p>
<p>The power station has an installed capacity of 62 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watt-peak" title="Watt-peak">MWp</a>, with more than 376,000 <a href="/wiki/Solar_panel" title="Solar panel">solar panels</a>. Approximately 190,000 panels (32 MW) are fitted on fixed structures, and 52,000 panels (10 MW) are fixed on <a href="/wiki/Solar_tracker" title="Solar tracker">single-axis trackers</a>.</p>
<p>A €7.6 million solar panel factory, located in Moura, was constructed by <a href="/wiki/Acciona" title="Acciona">Acciona</a>, which provided panels for Stage 2 of the station construction. Its future production will be targeted at the international market, with a capacity of producing 24 MW of solar panels annually.</p>
