<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Low Emissions Development Strategies Global Partnership</b> (LEDS GP) aims to advance climate-resilient low emission development and support transitions to a <a href="/wiki/Low-carbon_economy" title="Low-carbon economy">low-carbon economy</a> through coordination, information exchange and cooperation among countries and programs working to advance low-emission <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a>. The partnership was launched in 2011 and brings together more than 160 governmental and international institutions. The implementation, knowledge management, and outreach of LEDS GP is coordinated by a co-secretariat of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Climate_%26_Development_Knowledge_Network" title="Climate &amp; Development Knowledge Network">Climate &amp; Development Knowledge Network</a> (CDKN) and the <a href="/wiki/National_Renewable_Energy_Laboratory" title="National Renewable Energy Laboratory">National Renewable Energy Laboratory</a> (NREL).</p>
<p>LEDS GP delivers support through its three regional platforms, the Africa LEDS Partnership (AfLP), the <a class="external text" href="http://ledslac.org/" rel="nofollow">Latin America and the Caribbean Partnership</a> (LEDS LAC) and <a class="external text" href="http://asialeds.org/about-us" rel="nofollow">Asia Partnership</a> (ALP). It aims to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Strengthen support for low-emission climate-resilient development in all regions,</li>
<li>Mobilize capacity and advance peer-to-peer learning and collaboration on low emission climate-resilient development across countries, international institutions and practitioners, and</li>
<li>Improve and support coordination of low-emission climate-resilient development LEDS activities at the country, regional and global level.</li>
</ul>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Working_Groups">Working Groups</span></h2>
<p>LEDS GP delivers support through six technical working groups on the following topics.</p>
<ul>
<li>Agriculture, Forestry and Land Use (AFOLU)</li>
<li>Benefits Assessment of LEDS</li>
<li>Energy</li>
<li>Finance</li>
<li>Subnational Integration of LEDS</li>
<li>Transport</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technical_Assistance">Technical Assistance</span></h2>
<p>Through its <a class="external text" href="http://ledsgp.org/assistance/?loclang=en_gb" rel="nofollow">Remote Expert Assistance on LEDS</a> (REAL) service, the LEDS GP provides virtual technical assistance to developing country government agencies or initiatives—as well as their consultants, technical institutions, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-government_organizations" title="Non-government organizations">non-government organizations</a>—working on LEDS plans and implementation.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fellowship_Program">Fellowship Program</span></h2>
<p>In May 2016, LEDS GP announced the launch of its <a class="external text" href="http://ledsgp.org/2016/05/apply-2016-fellowship-program/?loclang=en_gb" rel="nofollow">Fellowship Program</a>, which aims to embed individuals or teams of practitioners in leading institutions to learn practical lessons that they can apply to low emission development strategies (LEDS) and/or <a href="/wiki/Intended_Nationally_Determined_Contributions" title="Intended Nationally Determined Contributions">Intended Nationally Determined Contributions</a> in their home countries.</p>
