<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>Substances, mixtures and exposure circumstances in this list have been classified by the <a href="/wiki/International_Agency_for_Research_on_Cancer" title="International Agency for Research on Cancer">IARC</a> as <i><b>Group 1</b>: The agent (mixture) is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carcinogenic" title="Carcinogenic">carcinogenic</a> to humans. The exposure circumstance entails exposures that are carcinogenic to humans.</i> This category is used when there is <i>sufficient evidence</i> of carcinogenicity in humans. Exceptionally, an agent (mixture) may be placed in this category when evidence of carcinogenicity in humans is less than sufficient but there is <i>sufficient evidence</i> of carcinogenicity in experimental animals and strong evidence in exposed humans that the agent (mixture) acts through a relevant mechanism of carcinogenicity.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span></h2>
<ol>
<li>This evaluation applies to the group of compounds as a whole and not necessarily to all individual compounds within the group.</li>
<li>Evaluated as a group.</li>
<li>There is also conclusive evidence that these agents have a protective effect against cancers of the ovary and endometrium.</li>
<li>Ethanol is not carcinogenic, but is metabolised to <a href="/wiki/Acetaldehyde" title="Acetaldehyde">acetaldehyde</a>, which is carcinogenic.</li>
<li>Specific radionuclides for which there is sufficient evidence for carcinogenicity to humans are also listed individually as Group 1 agents.</li>
<li>There is also conclusive evidence that this agent (<a href="/wiki/Tamoxifen" title="Tamoxifen">tamoxifen</a>) reduces the risk of contralateral breast cancer.</li>
</ol>
