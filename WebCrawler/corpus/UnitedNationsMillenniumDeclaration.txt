<p>On 8 September 2000, following a three-day <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Summit" title="Millennium Summit">Millennium Summit</a> of world leaders at the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">General Assembly</a> adopted the <b>Millennium Declaration</b>. A follow-up outcome of the resolution was passed by the General Assembly on 14 December 2000 to guide its implementation. Progress on implementation of the Declaration was reviewed at the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Summit" title="2005 World Summit">2005 World Summit</a> of leaders.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chapters">Chapters</span></h2>
<p>The Millennium Declaration has eight chapters and key objectives, adopted by 189 world leaders during the summit: The Declaration, after the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Declaration_and_Programme_of_Action" title="Vienna Declaration and Programme of Action">Vienna Declaration and Programme of Action</a>, stresses the observance of <a href="/wiki/International_human_rights_law" title="International human rights law">international human rights law</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_humanitarian_law" title="International humanitarian law">international humanitarian law</a> under the Principles of <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Charter" title="United Nations Charter">United Nations Charter</a> as well as the treaties on <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>. The Declaration also urges observance of the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_truce" title="Olympic truce">Olympic truce</a> individually and collectively.CITEREFUN2000</p>
