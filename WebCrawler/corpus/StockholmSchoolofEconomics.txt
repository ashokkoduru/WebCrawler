<div class="hatnote" role="note">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_school_(economics)" title="Stockholm school (economics)">Stockholm school (economics)</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>Stockholm School of Economics, SSE</b> (<a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>: <span lang="sv" xml:lang="sv"><i>Handelshögskolan i Stockholm</i></span><i>, HHS</i>) is one of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>'s leading <a href="/wiki/Business_school" title="Business school">business schools</a>. SSE is a privately owned business school that receives 78% of its financing from private sources and 22% from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_government" title="Swedish government">Swedish government</a>. SSE offers bachelors, masters and MBA programs, along with highly regarded PhD- and extensive Executive Education programs (customized and open programs).</p>
<p>SSE's Masters in Management program is ranked no. 33 worldwide by the <a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a>. <a href="/wiki/Quacquarelli_Symonds" title="Quacquarelli Symonds">QS</a> ranks SSE no.26 among universities in the field of economics worldwide.</p>
<p>SSE is accredited by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EQUIS" title="EQUIS">EQUIS</a> certifying that all of its main activities, teaching as well as research, are of the highest international standards. SSE is also the Swedish member institution of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CEMS_-_The_Global_Alliance_in_Management_Education" title="CEMS - The Global Alliance in Management Education">CEMS</a> together with universities such as <a href="/wiki/London_School_of_Economics" title="London School of Economics">London School of Economics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tsinghua_University" title="Tsinghua University">Tsinghua University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bocconi_University" title="Bocconi University">Bocconi University</a>, <a href="/wiki/HEC_Paris" title="HEC Paris">HEC Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/ESADE" title="ESADE">ESADE</a> Business School, and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_St._Gallen" title="University of St. Gallen">University of St. Gallen</a>.</p>
<p>SSE has founded sister organizations: <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_School_of_Economics_in_Riga" title="Stockholm School of Economics in Riga">SSE Riga</a> in <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_School_of_Economics_in_Russia" title="Stockholm School of Economics in Russia">SSE Russia</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Petersburg" title="St Petersburg">St Petersburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. It also operates <i>the European Institute of Japanese Studies</i> (<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">kanji</a>: 欧州日本研究所, Japanese, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romaji" title="Romaji">romaji</a>: Ōshū Nihon kenkyūjo), a research institute in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p>The Stockholm School of Economics was founded in 1909 on private initiative as a response to rapid industrialization and a growing need for well educated businessmen and company managers and has maintained close ties with the business community ever since. The foundation followed a substantial donation in 1903 by <a href="/wiki/Knut_Agathon_Wallenberg" title="Knut Agathon Wallenberg">Knut Agathon Wallenberg</a>. The name <i>handelshögskola</i> (roughly "college of commerce") was a parallel to the German term <i>Handelshochschule</i>, used by a number of German institutions started in the years before, commencing with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Handelshochschule_Leipzig" title="Handelshochschule Leipzig">Handelshochschule Leipzig</a> in 1898. The term <i>högskola</i> was at this time also established for specialised higher educational institutions outside the universities, such as the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Institute_of_Technology" title="Royal Institute of Technology">Royal Institute of Technology</a>, <i>(Kungl.) Tekniska högskolan</i>, which bore that name from 1877.</p>
<p>While founded as a business school, the subject of economics featured prominently in the research and curriculum of the school from the beginning.</p>
<p>The most well known scholars of the Stockholm School of Economics are arguably the economists <a href="/wiki/Eli_Heckscher" title="Eli Heckscher">Eli Heckscher</a> (<a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Statistics" title="Statistics">statistics</a> 1909–1929, professor of economic history 1929–1945), <a href="/wiki/Gunnar_Myrdal" title="Gunnar Myrdal">Gunnar Myrdal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bertil_Ohlin" title="Bertil Ohlin">Bertil Ohlin</a> (professors of economics). Heckscher is also known as the founder of <a href="/wiki/Economic_history" title="Economic history">economic history</a> as an independent academic discipline and his work <i>Svenskt Arbete och Liv</i> is a fundamental work within this subject.</p>
<p>Ohlin was also a leading figure within the school of doctrine with the same name, the so-called <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_school_(economics)" title="Stockholm school (economics)">Stockholm school</a>; a group of leading <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavian</a> economists influenced by <a href="/wiki/Knut_Wicksell" title="Knut Wicksell">Knut Wicksell</a>, most of them active in Stockholm, either at the Stockholm School of Economics or the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stockholm_University_College" title="Stockholm University College">Stockholm University College</a>. This school of doctrine was to have a profound influence on post-WWII Swedish economic policy and the development of the modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scandinavian_welfare_model" title="Scandinavian welfare model">Scandinavian</a> <a href="/wiki/Welfare_state" title="Welfare state">Welfare state</a>. Heckscher and Ohlin jointly developed the so-called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heckscher-Ohlin_model" title="Heckscher-Ohlin model">Heckscher-Ohlin theory</a></i>, the standard international mathematical model of international trade. Gunnar Myrdal received the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Memorial_Prize_in_Economic_Sciences" title="Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences">Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences</a> in 1974 (shared with his ideological <i><a href="/wiki/Archenemy" title="Archenemy">nemesis</a></i>, Austrian economist <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Hayek" title="Friedrich Hayek">Friedrich Hayek</a>); Bertil Ohlin received the Nobel Prize in Economics in 1977 (shared with British economist <a href="/wiki/James_Meade" title="James Meade">James Meade</a>). Other prominent members of the Stockholm school were the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_University" title="Stockholm University">Stockholm University</a> professor <a href="/wiki/Gustav_Cassel" title="Gustav Cassel">Gustav Cassel</a>, who developed standard economic theory of <a href="/wiki/Purchasing_power_parity" title="Purchasing power parity">Purchasing power parity</a> and economist <a href="/wiki/Dag_Hammarskj%C3%B6ld" title="Dag Hammarskjöld">Dag Hammarskjöld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secretary-General_of_the_United_Nations" title="Secretary-General of the United Nations">Secretary-General of the United Nations</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, United States.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academics">Academics</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Admission">Admission</span></h3>
<p>For Master programmes, applicants have to have a GMAT score of over 600 and a TOEFL iBT score of over 100 in order to be considered suitable for applying. In the academic year 2012/2013 the university received 3261 applications for the four Masters programmes which it offered at the time. Therefore, the according acceptance rate would have been low.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Programs">Programs</span></h3>
<p>Stockholm School of Economics offers the following programs:</p>
<ul>
<li>Bachelor of Science (BSc) in Business and Economics</li>
<li>Bachelor of Science (BSc) in Retail Management</li>
<li>Master of Science (MSc) in Business &amp; Management</li>
<li>Master of Science (MSc) in Finance &amp; Accounting</li>
<li>Master of Science (MSc) in Economics</li>
<li>Master of Science (MSc) in General Management</li>
<li>Doctoral (PhD) Program with three specializations (Business Administration, Economics, Finance)</li>
<li>MBA Program (offered in executive format)</li>
</ul>
<p>The MSc programs are all conducted in English.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="SSE_Master_of_Science_in_Business_and_Management">SSE Master of Science in Business and Management</span></h4>
<p>The Master of Science in Business and Management is a two-year program. (120 ECTS credits) There are offered three specializations: International Business (CEMS), Management and Marketing &amp; Media Management. Within their specialization, students write a Master's thesis worth 30 ECTS credits.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="SSE_Master_of_Science_in_General_Management">SSE Master of Science in General Management</span></h4>
<p>The MSc in General Management is a two-year business program targeting students with bachelor's degrees in subjects other than Business or Economics, i.e. engineering, social science law or medicine.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="SSE_Master_of_Science_in_Economics">SSE Master of Science in Economics</span></h4>
<p>The in Economics is a program designed for students with a background in economics or business. As well as the other master programs it is a two-year program with 120 ECTS. There are offered two specializations: Applied Economic Analysis and International Economics.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="SSE_Master_of_Science_in_Finance_.26_Accounting">SSE Master of Science in Finance &amp; Accounting</span></h4>
<p>The MSc in Finance and Accounting is also a two-year program (120 ECTS). There are offered three different specializations: Investment Management, Corporate Finance and Accounting &amp; Financial Management.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="SSE_Masters_of_Business_Administration_.28MBA.29.2C_Executive_Format">SSE Masters of Business Administration (MBA), Executive Format</span></h4>
<p>The SSE EMBA program was launched in 2001. Since 2001, the year the Financial Times began its Executive MBA ranking, the SSE Executive MBA has been the first in the Nordic league. Worldwide its average rank in the last three years was 56.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="SSE_PhD_in_Business_Administration.2C_Economics.2C_Finance">SSE PhD in Business Administration, Economics, Finance</span></h4>
<p>The SSE PhD Program was launched more than 60 years ago and has graduated more than 500 PhDs. There are three separate PhD programs at SSE:</p>
<ul>
<li>Business Administration</li>
<li>Economics</li>
<li>Finance</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alumni">Alumni</span></h2>
<p>Stockholm School of Economics (SSE) alumni are defined as previous students that have graduated from one of SSE’s degree programs. Today, there are some 14 000 alumni in this network. In addition to the alumni are the so-called <i>SSE IFL Networkers,</i> previous participants at IFL open or custom programs, these sum up to an additional 10 000.</p>
<p>SSE maintains contacts with its alumni in a number of different ways; both through direct and indirect contacts and through various types of communication channels. Contacts are administered through the <i>SSE Alumni Office.</i></p>
<p>Today, many alumni are involved in the school's advisory board, the alumni association's board, the Student Association <i>et al.</i>, all aligned to SSE activities. Even more alumni return to the School as guest lectures, or sponsors of course projects, as suppliers of internship opportunities, or as recruiters from large companies.</p>
<p>Currently, if an alumnus wishes to connect with another SSE graduate, they have five primary options: 1. Attending the alumni events arranged by SSE Alumni Office 2. Networking through the <i>SSE Alumninet,</i> a web portal open to holders of an SSE diploma. Alumninet had some 14 000 members as of January 2013, or through the official LinkedIn groups maintained by SSE 3. Membership in <i>the Alumni Association of the Stockholm School of Economics</i> – an alumni association sponsored and organized by alumni for Swedish-speaking graduates of SSE – or by joining <i>the American Friends of SSE</i> – an affiliated alumni group, based in the US. 4. Joining any of the unofficial alumni groups and networks, maintained by the alumni themselves, e.g. in the UK.</p>
