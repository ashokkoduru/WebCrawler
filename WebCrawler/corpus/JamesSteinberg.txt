<p><b>James Braidy "Jim" Steinberg</b> (born May 7, 1953) is an American academic and political advisor, and former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deputy_Secretary_of_State" title="Deputy Secretary of State">Deputy Secretary of State</a>. He is currently a Professor of Social Science, International Affairs, and Law at the <a href="/wiki/Maxwell_School_of_Citizenship_and_Public_Affairs" title="Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs">Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs</a> at <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_University" title="Syracuse University">Syracuse University</a>.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span></h3>
<p>Steinberg was born to a <a href="/wiki/American_Jews" title="American Jews">Jewish</a> family in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a>. He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Phillips_Academy" title="Phillips Academy">Phillips Academy</a> (1970), <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a> (1973), and <a href="/wiki/Yale_Law_School" title="Yale Law School">Yale Law School</a> (1978). His previous positions included a senior fellowship for US Strategic Policy at the <a href="/wiki/International_Institute_for_Strategic_Studies" title="International Institute for Strategic Studies">International Institute for Strategic Studies</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, UK (1985–1987), and senior analyst at <a href="/wiki/RAND_Corporation" title="RAND Corporation">RAND Corporation</a> (1989–1993). Steinberg also served as a Senior Advisor to the Markle Foundation (2000-2001) and was a member of the Markle Task Force on National Security in the Information Age.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Carter_campaign">Carter campaign</span></h3>
<p>During the national elections which brought U.S. President <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Carter" title="Jimmy Carter">Jimmy Carter</a> into office, Steinberg worked on the presidential campaign of the Carter-Mondale ticket.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Clinton_administration">Clinton administration</span></h3>
<p>Steinberg served as US State Department <a href="/wiki/Director_of_Policy_Planning" title="Director of Policy Planning">Director of Policy Planning</a> (1994–1996), then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deputy_National_Security_Advisor" title="Deputy National Security Advisor">Deputy National Security Advisor</a> (December 1996 – 2001) to US President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a>. He also served on the <a href="/wiki/Project_on_National_Security_Reform" title="Project on National Security Reform">Project on National Security Reform</a>'s Guiding Coalition.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brookings_Institution_Director_and_Lyndon_B._Johnson_School_Dean">Brookings Institution Director and Lyndon B. Johnson School Dean</span></h3>
<p>After serving in the Clinton administration, Steinberg was a senior fellow at the <a href="/wiki/Brookings_Institution" title="Brookings Institution">Brookings Institution</a> in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> and the Institution's vice president and director of Foreign Policy Studies (2001–2005). Steinberg was then Dean of the <a href="/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson_School_of_Public_Affairs" title="Lyndon B. Johnson School of Public Affairs">Lyndon B. Johnson School of Public Affairs</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas_at_Austin" title="University of Texas at Austin">University of Texas at Austin</a> (2006–2009) until his appointment as U.S. Deputy Secretary of State on January 28, 2009, taking a leave of absence from the School for the duration of his term in office.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Obama_administration">Obama administration</span></h3>

<p>According to the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Journal" title="Wall Street Journal">Wall Street Journal</a></i>, Steinberg, along with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daniel_Kurtzer" title="Daniel Kurtzer">Daniel Kurtzer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Ross" title="Dennis Ross">Dennis Ross</a>, were among the principal authors of <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>’s address on the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> to AIPAC in June 2008, which was viewed as the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic Party</a> nominee’s most expansive on <a href="/wiki/International_relations" title="International relations">international affairs</a>.</p>
<p>He was mentioned as being "at the top" of Obama's list of candidates for the post of <a href="/wiki/National_Security_Advisor_(United_States)" title="National Security Advisor (United States)">National Security Advisor</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Mitchell" title="Andrea Mitchell">Andrea Mitchell</a> reported on November 24, 2008 that Hillary Clinton would appoint Steinberg <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deputy_Secretary_of_State" title="Deputy Secretary of State">Deputy Secretary of State</a>. On December 23, 2008, Steinberg himself confirmed the appointment in a letter addressed to students and faculty at the Johnson school.</p>
<p>As Deputy Secretary of State and principal Deputy to US Secretary of State <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a>, Steinberg notably coined the phrase "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Strategic_reassurance&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Strategic reassurance (page does not exist)">strategic reassurance</a>" to describe <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sino-American_relations" title="Sino-American relations">U.S.-China relations</a> suggestive of the idea that the United States should reassure China about welcoming China's rise while China would reassure the US and its neighbors that it would not conflict with their interests.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Israel-U.S._Strategic_Dialogue">Israel-U.S. Strategic Dialogue</span></h3>
<p>In October 2010, Steinberg met with <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel’s</a> Deputy Foreign Minister, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Ayalon" title="Danny Ayalon">Danny Ayalon</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>, where they discussed how to improve regional security and stability through boosting and growing the already strong cooperation between their two nations. During the talks, both delegates expressed their commitment to a lasting peace between Israel and its neighbors and their grave concern regarding Iran’s continued non-compliance with its international obligations through pursuit of a military nuclear program.</p>
<p>The following spring, they met in Jerusalem where they again took advantage of the opportunity to work together to identify and strategize against the threats both countries face including the rapidly changing political situation in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and the ongoing Iranian nuclear program.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Maxwell_School_Deanship">Maxwell School Deanship</span></h3>
<p>In March 2011, Steinberg was named Dean of the <a href="/wiki/Maxwell_School_of_Citizenship_and_Public_Affairs" title="Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs">Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs</a> at <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_University" title="Syracuse University">Syracuse University</a>. On July 28, 2011, he resigned as Deputy Secretary of State and assumed his new position.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Think_Tanks">Think Tanks</span></h3>
<p>Steinberg is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Council_on_Foreign_Relations" title="Council on Foreign Relations">Council on Foreign Relations</a>. He has participated in 8 conferences of the <a href="/wiki/Bilderberg_Group" title="Bilderberg Group">Bilderberg Group</a> (1994, 2000, 2002, 2003, 2006 and 2009-2011).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>He is married to Sherburne B. Abbott, the director of the Center for Science and Practice of Sustainability at the University of Texas at Austin. They have two daughters: Jenna and Emma.</p>
