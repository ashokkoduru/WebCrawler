<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">

<p><b>Georg Ludwig Hartig</b> (September 2, 1764 – February 2, 1837) was a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forester</a>.</p>
<p></p>

<p></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span></h2>
<p>Hartig was born at <a href="/wiki/Gladenbach" title="Gladenbach">Gladenbach</a>, in present-day <a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a>. After obtaining a practical knowledge of forestry from his uncle at <a href="/wiki/Harzburg" title="Harzburg">Harzburg</a>, he studied from 1781 to 1783 at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Giessen" title="University of Giessen">University of Giessen</a>, which had commenced a course of instruction in forestry just a few years earlier, in 1778.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<p>In 1786, Hartwig was appointed as Manager of Forests for the Prince of <a href="/wiki/Solms-Braunfels" title="Solms-Braunfels">Solms-Braunfels</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hungen" title="Hungen">Hungen</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Wetterau" title="Wetterau">Wetterau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a>. While in this position, he founded a school for the teaching of <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a>, one of the first dedicated schools of forestry in Europe.</p>
<p>After a decade in Hungen, in 1797, he received an appointment as Inspector of Forests to the Prince of <a href="/wiki/House_of_Orange-Nassau" title="House of Orange-Nassau">Orange-Nassau</a> and moved to <a href="/wiki/Dillenburg" title="Dillenburg">Dillenburg</a>, continuing his school of forestry there. Attendance increased considerably in Dillenburg. On the dissolution of the principality by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleon I of France</a> in 1805 he lost his position.</p>
<p>In 1806, Hartig went to <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a> as Chief Inspector of Forests. Five years later, in 1811, he was called to <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> in a similar capacity. There he reestablished his school once again, succeeding in connecting it with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Berlin" title="University of Berlin">University of Berlin</a>.</p>
<p>Hartwig received an appointment as Honorary Professor at the University of Berlin in 1830. He died at Berlin on 2 February 1837.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sons">Sons</span></h2>
<p>His son <a href="/wiki/Theodor_Hartig" title="Theodor Hartig">Theodor Hartig</a> (1805–1880) and grandson <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hartig" title="Robert Hartig">Robert Hartig</a> (1839–1901) also were distinguished for their contributions to the study of forestry.</p>

<li><i>Anweisung zur Holzzucht für Förster</i>, Marburg 1791 (Directions for Wood-breeding for Foresters)</li>
<li><i>Physicalische Versuche über das Verhältniß der Brennbarkeit der meisten deutschen Wald-Baum-Hölzer...etc.</i>, 1794</li>
<li><i>Anweisung zur Taxation der Forste oder zur Bestimmung des Holzertrags der Wälder...etc.</i>, Gießen 1795</li>
<li><i>Grundsätze der Forst-Direktion</i>, Hadamer: Neue Gelehrten Buchhandlung 1803</li>
<li><i>Lehrbuch für Förster und die es werden wollen...etc.</i>,(3 vols.), Stuttgart 1808 (Textbook for Foresters and ...)</li>
<li><i>Kubiktabellen für geschnittene, beschlagene und runde Hölzer...etc.</i>, 1815 (4th ed. Berlin and Elbing, 1837; 10th ed. Berlin, 1871)</li>
<li><i>Lehrbuch für Jäger und die es werden wollen...etc.</i>, (2 vols.), Stuttgart 1810/1812 (Textbook for Hunters and...)</li>
<li><i>Beitrag zur Lehre von der Ablösung der Holz-, Streu- und Weideservituten</i>, Berlin 1829</li>
<li><i>Die Forstwissenschaft in ihrem Umfange...etc.</i>, Berlin 1831 (Forest Science in its Scope...)</li>
<li><i>Lexikon für Jäger und Jagdfreunde oder waidmännisches Conversations-Lexikon</i>, 1836 (2nd ed. Berlin, 1859–1861) (Lexicon for Hunters and Hunting Companions or the Country-Sportsman's Conversations-Lexicon)</li>
</ul>
