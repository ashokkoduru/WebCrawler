pasture from the latin pastus past participle of pascere to feed is land used for grazing pasture lands in the narrow sense are enclosed tracts of farmland grazed by domesticated livestock such as horses cattle sheep or swine the vegetation of tended pasture forage consists mainly of grasses with an interspersion of legumes and other forbs non-grass herbaceous plants pasture is typically grazed throughout the summer in contrast to meadow which is ungrazed or used for grazing only after being mown to make hay for animal fodder pasture in a wider sense additionally includes rangelands other unenclosed pastoral systems and land types used by wild animals for grazing or browsing pasture lands in the narrow sense are distinguished from rangelands by being managed through more intensive agricultural practices of seeding irrigation and the use of fertilizers while rangelands grow primarily native vegetation managed with extensive practices like controlled burning and regulated
